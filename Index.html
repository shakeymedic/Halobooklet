DAS GUIDELINES 2025

Surgical Airway (eFONA)

Can't Intubate, Can't Oxygenate (CICO)

1
DECLARE EMERGENCY
  • Call out: "CICO. Proceeding to Emergency Front of Neck Airway."
  • Action: 100% Oxygen to face/nose. Extend Neck.
  • Human Factors: Plan D is a rescue procedure. Do not delay.
2
LARYNGEAL HANDSHAKE
  • Use non-dominant hand. Grasp Hyoid and Thyroid lamina.
  • Slide index finger down to Cricothyroid Membrane.
  • Plan: Scalpel - Bougie - Tube.
KIT: Scalpel (Size 10) • Bougie • Size 6.0 ETT
3
INCISION (SCALPEL)
  • Transverse Stab through CTM (Blade edge towards you).
  • Turn Blade 90° (Sharp edge caudal/towards feet).
  • DO NOT REMOVE SCALPEL. Swap hands if needed to hold tract.
4
RAILROAD (BOUGIE & TUBE)
  • Slide Bougie down the flat of the blade into trachea.
  • Remove Scalpel carefully.
  • Railroad lubricated Size 6.0 ETT over bougie.
  • Twist tube to avoid snagging. Inflate cuff.
5
CONFIRMATION
  • Connect BVM & Ventilate.
  • Mandatory: Confirm EtCO2 Waveform.
  • Suction trachea (blood is common). Secure tube.
Reference: Difficult Airway Society (DAS) 2025 Guidelines: Management of Unanticipated Difficult Intubation.
OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY

Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Perimortem C-Section (>20 Weeks)

!
GOAL: DELIVERY < 5 MINS
  • Indication: Maternal Arrest. Uterus palpable ≥ Umbilicus.
  • Location: Operate on the trolley. DO NOT move to theatre.
  • Timing: Incision at Minute 4 if no ROSC.
1
CPR MODIFICATIONS
  • Manual Uterine Displacement to LEFT (Relieve IVC).
  • Splash Chlorhexidine (No bone prep/drapes).
  • Allocated team member for Neonatal Resus.
2
THE INCISION
  • Vertical Midline Incision: Xiphisternum to Pubis.
  • Incise deep through skin/fat to rectus sheath.
  • Separate rectus muscles (bluntly or cut midline).
  • Open peritoneum (watch for bowel/bladder).
3
UTERINE ENTRY
  • Vertical incision in lower uterine segment.
  • Insert fingers to lift uterine wall away from fetal head.
  • Extend incision vertically with Trauma Shears.
  • DELIVER BABY -> Clamp/Cut Cord -> Pass to Neo.
4
POST-DELIVERY
  • Deliver Placenta manually. Pack uterus.
  • Close uterus (Running large absorbable suture).
  • Drugs: Oxytocin 10iu IV, Antibiotics, TXA 1g.
Reference: Resuscitation Council UK (2025 Update) & FPHC Consensus Statement on Obstetric Emergencies.
TRAUMA ARREST

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

"The Clamshell" Approach

!
INDICATIONS (2025)
  • Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins. Signs of Life on scene.
  • Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins. Witnessed. Signs of Life on scene.
  • Goals: Release Tamponade, Control Heart Bleed, Clamp Aorta.
1
INCISION (CLAMSHELL)
  • Stop CPR briefly. Intubate Right Main Bronchus if possible.
  • 5th Intercostal Space (Inframammary fold).
  • Incise: Mid-Axillary Line to Mid-Axillary Line (Bow tie).
  • Cut through intercostals with heavy scissors.
2
EXPOSURE
  • Cut Sternum transversely with Gigli saw or Shears.
  • Lift chest wall ("Open the Clamshell"). Retract fully.
  • Open Pericardium longitudinally (Anterior to Phrenic Nerve).
3
ACTIONS
  • Evacuate clots. Deliver heart.
  • Control Bleeding: Finger occlusion / Staples / Foley / Sutures.
  • Internal Massage: Two hands, clapping motion (Flat palms).
4
AORTIC CROSS CLAMP
  • Retract left lung medially.
  • Identify Descending Aorta against the spine.
  • Blunt dissect and apply vascular clamp to maximise cerebral flow.
Reference: FPHC & RCEM Consensus Statement on Traumatic Cardiac Arrest (2025).
BURNS EMERGENCY

Escharotomy

Circumferential Full Thickness Burns

!
INDICATIONS
  • Chest: Ventilation compromise / High Peak Pressures.
  • Limbs: Ischaemia (Pallor, CRT >2s, Absent Doppler, Pain).
  • Goal: Release constriction. Edges should gape visibly.
1
CHEST ("ROMAN BREASTPLATE")
  • Vertical: Bilateral Anterior Axillary Lines.
  • Transverse Top: Connect lines below clavicles.
  • Transverse Bottom: Connect lines at costal margin.
  • Ensure incisions extend into unburned skin if possible.
2
UPPER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral: Shoulder to wrist.
  • Mid-Medial: Axilla to wrist.
  • Caution: Medial Elbow (Ulnar Nerve) - Cut Anterior to Epicondyle.
  • Release Thenar/Hypothenar eminences if hands involved.
3
LOWER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral: Hip to ankle.
  • Mid-Medial: Groin to ankle.
  • Caution: Fibular Head (Common Peroneal Nerve).
Reference: British Burn Association (BBA) & LSEBN Guidelines (2025 Review).
OPHTHALMIC EMERGENCY

Lateral Canthotomy

Retrobulbar Haematoma

!
INDICATIONS
  • Proptosis + Trauma + Decreased VA / APD.
  • IOP > 40 mmHg (or 'Rock Hard' globe).
  • Time is Vision. Do not delay for CT imaging.
1
PREPARE
  • Irrigate eye. Local Anaesthetic w/ Adrenaline to lateral canthus.
  • Kit: Mosquito Forceps, Iris/Tenotomy Scissors, Forceps.
  • Aim needle away from globe.
2
CRUSH & CUT
  • Crush lateral canthus with Mosquito Forceps (1 min) for haemostasis.
  • Cut the crushed tissue (Skin & Orbicularis) 1-2cm towards ear.
3
CANTHOLYSIS (CRITICAL)
  • Pull lower lid down to reveal Inferior Crus (Tendinous band).
  • "Strum" with scissors to identify.
  • Cut Inferior Crus. Lower lid should hang loose.
  • Re-check IOP. If still high -> Cut Superior Crus.
Reference: RCEM & Royal College of Ophthalmologists Consensus (2025).
THORACIC EMERGENCY

Chest Drain (Open)

Trauma / Haemothorax

1
SAFETY TRIANGLE
  • Lat Dorsi (Lat). Pec Major (Med). Axilla (Base).
  • Target: 5th Intercostal Space, Anterior Axillary Line.
  • Standard: Use Ultrasound to mark site if stable/available.
2
INCISION & DISSECTION
  • Local Anaesthetic (Skin to Pleura).
  • 3cm Transverse skin incision with scalpel.
  • Blunt dissection with Kelly Forceps through intercostals.
  • Stay on Upper Border of Rib (Avoid NV bundle).
3
PLEURAL ENTRY
  • Push forceps through pleura ("Pop").
  • Open forceps widely to dilate tract.
  • Finger Sweep: Confirm space, clear adhesions/clot.
4
INSERTION
  • Insert Large Bore Tube (28-32F for Trauma).
  • Aim Apical & Posterior.
  • Connect to seal. Suture (Mattress). Tape. CXR.
Reference: British Thoracic Society (BTS) & RCEM Guidelines (2025).
GI HAEMORRHAGE

Sengstaken / Minnesota

Uncontrollable Variceal Bleed

1
PREPARATION
  • Intubate First. Airway protection is mandatory.
  • Test balloons for leaks underwater. Fully deflate.
  • Lubricate generously.
2
INSERTION & SAFETY CHECK
  • Insert to 50cm. Check gastric aspiration.
  • Inflate Gastric Balloon with 50ml Air ONLY initially.
  • STOP & CXR. Confirm balloon is below diaphragm.
  • If confirmed: Inflate to 250ml (or device max). Clamp.
Warning: Full inflation in oesophagus = Rupture/Death.
3
TRACTION
  • Gently pull back until resistance felt.
  • Apply 1kg Traction (e.g., 1L Saline bag over pulley).
  • This usually stops the bleeding (compression of GE junction).
4
OESOPHAGEAL BALLOON
  • Rescue Only. Use if bleeding continues despite traction.
  • Inflate to 30-40 mmHg (Manometer essential).
  • Release pressure regularly to prevent necrosis.
Reference: British Society of Gastroenterology (BSG) Guidelines 2025.
MAX-FAX TRAUMA

Severe Max-Fax Bleed

Stabilisation

!
AIRWAY
  • Conscious: Sit up, lean forward.
  • Unconscious: Intubate (RSI with Double Setup). Avoid nasal route.
  • Consider eFONA early if anatomy destroyed.
1
MIDFACE REDUCTION
  • Bleeding is often venous from Pterygoid Plexus (cannot clamp).
  • Action: Gloved fingers behind upper teeth/alveolus.
  • Pull Forward to disimpact the floating face.
  • Use Bite Block or Gag to maintain position.
2
NASAL PACKING
  • Rapid Rhino: Bilateral 7.5cm / 9cm packs.
  • Foley Catheter: Insert, inflate 15ml, pull back to choanae, clamp. Pack anterior nose with gauze.
3
MANDIBLE & ADJUNCTS
  • "Flail Mandible" -> Tongue obstruction. Pull jaw forward.
  • TXA 1g IV (or per local Massive Haemorrhage Protocol).
  • Activate MHP. ROTEM guided replacement.
Reference: RCEM & FPHC Consensus Statement on Maxillofacial Trauma (2025).
