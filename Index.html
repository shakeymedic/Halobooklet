DAS GUIDELINES 2025

Surgical Airway (eFONA)

Can't Intubate, Can't Oxygenate (CICO)

1
DECLARE EMERGENCY
  • Call out: "CICO. Proceeding to Emergency Front of Neck Airway."
  • Action: 100% Oxygen to face. Extend Neck.
  • Mindset: Plan D is a rescue procedure. Do not delay.
2
LARYNGEAL HANDSHAKE
  • Use non-dominant hand. Grasp Hyoid and Thyroid.
  • Slide index finger down to Cricothyroid Membrane.
  • Plan: Scalpel - Bougie - Tube.
KIT: Scalpel (Size 10) • Bougie • Size 6.0 ETT
3
INCISION (SCALPEL)
  • Transverse Stab through CTM (Blade edge towards you).
  • Turn Blade 90° (Sharp edge caudal/towards feet).
  • DO NOT REMOVE SCALPEL. Swap hands if needed.
4
RAILROAD (BOUGIE & TUBE)
  • Slide Bougie down the blade into trachea.
  • Remove Scalpel carefully.
  • Railroad lubricated Size 6.0 ETT over bougie.
  • Twist tube to avoid snagging. Inflate cuff.
5
CONFIRMATION
  • Connect BVM & Ventilate.
  • Mandatory: Confirm EtCO2 Waveform.
  • Suction trachea. Secure tube.
Reference: Difficult Airway Society (DAS) 2025 Guidelines.
OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY

Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Perimortem C-Section (>20 Weeks)

!
GOAL: DELIVERY < 5 MINS
  • Indication: Maternal Arrest. Uterus ≥ Umbilicus.
  • Location: Operate on the trolley. DO NOT move to theatre.
  • Timing: Incision at Minute 4 if no ROSC.
1
CPR MODIFICATIONS
  • Manual Uterine Displacement to LEFT.
  • Splash Chlorhexidine (No bone prep).
  • Allocated team member for Neonatal Resus.
2
THE INCISION
  • Vertical Midline: Xiphisternum to Pubis.
  • Incise deep through skin/fat to rectus sheath.
  • Separate rectus muscles. Open peritoneum.
3
UTERINE ENTRY
  • Vertical incision in lower uterine segment.
  • Lift uterine wall away from fetal head.
  • Extend incision with Shears.
  • DELIVER BABY -> Clamp/Cut Cord -> Pass to Neo.
4
POST-DELIVERY
  • Deliver Placenta manually. Pack uterus.
  • Close uterus (Running absorbable suture).
  • Drugs: Oxytocin 10iu, Antibiotics, TXA 1g.
Reference: Resuscitation Council UK (2025 Update).
TRAUMA ARREST

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

"The Clamshell" Approach

!
INDICATIONS (2025)
  • Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins. Signs of Life on scene.
  • Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins. Witnessed. Signs of Life on scene.
  • Goals: Relieve Tamponade, Control Heart Bleed, Clamp Aorta.
1
INCISION (CLAMSHELL)
  • Stop CPR briefly. Intubate Right Main Bronchus if possible.
  • 5th Intercostal Space (Inframammary fold).
  • Incise: Mid-Axillary Line to Mid-Axillary Line (Bow tie).
2
EXPOSURE
  • Cut Sternum with Gigli saw or Shears.
  • Lift chest wall ("Open the Clamshell"). Retract fully.
  • Open Pericardium longitudinally (Anterior to Phrenic Nerve).
3
ACTIONS
  • Evacuate clots. Deliver heart.
  • Control Bleeding: Finger occlusion / Staples / Foley.
  • Internal Massage: Two hands, clapping motion.
4
AORTIC CROSS CLAMP
  • Retract left lung medially.
  • Identify Descending Aorta against spine.
  • Apply vascular clamp to maximise cerebral flow.
Reference: FPHC & RCEM Consensus Statement on Traumatic Cardiac Arrest (2025).
BURNS EMERGENCY

Escharotomy

Circumferential Full Thickness Burns

!
INDICATIONS
  • Chest: Ventilation compromise / High Peak Pressures.
  • Limbs: Ischaemia (Pallor, CRT >2s, Absent Doppler).
  • Goal: Release constriction. Edges should gape.
1
CHEST ("ROMAN BREASTPLATE")
  • Vertical: Bilateral Anterior Axillary Lines.
  • Transverse Top: Connect lines below clavicles.
  • Transverse Bottom: Connect lines at costal margin.
2
UPPER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral: Shoulder to wrist.
  • Mid-Medial: Axilla to wrist.
  • Caution: Medial Elbow (Ulnar Nerve) - Cut Anterior to Epicondyle.
3
LOWER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral: Hip to ankle.
  • Mid-Medial: Groin to ankle.
  • Caution: Fibular Head (Common Peroneal Nerve).
Reference: LSEBN Guidelines (2025).
OPHTHALMIC EMERGENCY

Lateral Canthotomy

Retrobulbar Haematoma

!
INDICATIONS
  • Proptosis + Trauma + Decreased VA / APD.
  • IOP > 40 mmHg (or 'Rock Hard' globe).
  • Time is Vision. Do not delay for CT.
1
PREPARE
  • Irrigate eye. Local Anaesthetic w/ Adrenaline.
  • Kit: Mosquito Forceps, Iris Scissors, Forceps.
2
CRUSH & CUT
  • Crush lateral canthus with Mosquito Forceps (1 min).
  • Cut the crushed tissue (Skin & Orbicularis) 1-2cm towards ear.
3
CANTHOLYSIS (CRITICAL)
  • Pull lower lid down to reveal Inferior Crus.
  • "Strum" with scissors to identify.
  • Cut Inferior Crus. Lower lid should hang loose.
  • Re-check IOP. If high -> Cut Superior Crus.
Reference: RCEM & Royal College of Ophthalmologists Consensus (2025).
THORACIC EMERGENCY

Chest Drain (Open)

Trauma / Haemothorax

1
SAFETY TRIANGLE
  • Lat Dorsi. Pec Major. Axilla.
  • Target: 5th Intercostal Space, Anterior Axillary Line.
  • Use Ultrasound to mark site if stable.
2
INCISION & DISSECTION
  • Local Anaesthetic (Skin to Pleura).
  • 3cm Transverse skin incision.
  • Blunt dissection with Kelly Forceps.
  • Stay on Upper Border of Rib.
3
PLEURAL ENTRY
  • Push forceps through pleura ("Pop").
  • Open forceps widely to dilate tract.
  • Finger Sweep: Confirm space/adhesions.
4
INSERTION
  • Insert Large Bore Tube (28-32F).
  • Aim Apical & Posterior.
  • Connect to seal. Suture. Tape. CXR.
Reference: BTS & RCEM Guidelines (2025).
VASCULAR ACCESS

Subclavian Line

Infraclavicular Landmark Approach

!
NOTE
  • Use Ultrasound if available. This guide is for crash scenarios.
  • Right side preferred.
1
POSITION
  • Trendelenburg 15°. Rolled towel between scapulae.
  • Head neutral or turned slightly away.
2
LANDMARKS
  • Junction of Medial and Middle thirds of clavicle.
  • Index finger in Sternal Notch.
  • Insert needle 1cm below clavicle.
  • Aim: Sternal Notch. Keep flat.
3
SELDINGER
  • Venous Flash -> Stabilise.
  • Insert Wire (Check ECG for ectopics).
  • Dilate -> Catheter -> Aspirate -> Suture.
  • Mandatory CXR.
Reference: AAGBI Safe Vascular Access.
GI HAEMORRHAGE

Sengstaken / Minnesota

Uncontrollable Variceal Bleed

1
PREPARATION
  • Intubate First. Airway protection is mandatory.
  • Test balloons underwater. Lubricate.
2
INSERTION & SAFETY
  • Insert to 50cm. Check gastric aspiration.
  • Inflate Gastric Balloon with 50ml Air ONLY.
  • STOP & CXR. Confirm balloon below diaphragm.
  • If confirmed: Inflate to 250ml. Clamp.
Warning: Oesophageal inflation = Rupture/Death.
3
TRACTION
  • Gently pull back until resistance felt.
  • Apply 1kg Traction (e.g., 1L Saline bag).
4
OESOPHAGEAL BALLOON
  • Rescue Only. Use if bleeding continues.
  • Inflate to 30-40 mmHg (Manometer essential).
Reference: BSG Guidelines 2025.
MAX-FAX TRAUMA

Severe Max-Fax Bleed

Stabilisation

!
AIRWAY
  • Conscious: Sit up, lean forward.
  • Unconscious: Intubate (RSI). Avoid nasal route.
  • Consider eFONA early.
1
MIDFACE REDUCTION
  • Action: Gloved fingers behind upper teeth.
  • Pull Forward to disimpact the floating face.
  • Use Bite Block or Gag to maintain position.
2
NASAL PACKING
  • Rapid Rhino: Bilateral.
  • Foley Catheter: Insert, inflate 15ml, pull back, clamp. Pack anterior.
3
ADJUNCTS
  • TXA 1g IV.
  • Activate MHP. ROTEM guided replacement.
Reference: RCEM & FPHC Consensus (2025).
OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY

Emergency Breech

Vaginal Delivery Manoeuvres

!
THE GOLDEN RULE
  • HANDS OFF THE BREECH.
  • Allow breech to hang. Call Obs/Paeds.
1
LEGS STUCK (Pinard's)
  • Finger in popliteal fossa.
  • Flex knee laterally (abduct). Leg will flex out.
2
ARMS STUCK (Loveset's)
  • Signs: Winged Scapula.
  • Hold bony pelvis. Rotate 180°. Arm sweeps down.
  • Rotate back 180° for other arm.
3
HEAD STUCK (MSV)
  • Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit:
  • Lay baby on forearm. Finger in mouth (flex jaw).
  • Other hand on shoulders. Flex head. Lift up.
Reference: RCOG Green-top 20b / PROMPT 2024.
ANAESTHETIC EMERGENCY

LAST (Lipid Rescue)

Local Anaesthetic Toxicity

!
RECOGNITION
  • CNS: Tingling, metallic taste, seizure.
  • CVS: Bradycardia, Broad QRS, Arrest.
  • Action: STOP INJECTION. Call for Lipid.
1
AIRWAY & CPR
  • Intubate. Hyperventilate (alkalosis).
  • Prolonged CPR. Avoid Lidocaine.
2
INTRALIPID 20% (BOLUS)
  • Bolus: 1.5 ml/kg over 1 minute.
  • 70kg Adult = 100 ml.
  • Repeat q5min if unstable (Max 3).
3
INTRALIPID 20% (INFUSION)
  • Rate: 15 ml/kg/hour.
  • 70kg Adult = 1000 ml/hour.
  • Double rate if unstable.
Reference: AAGBI Safety Guideline 2023.
CARDIAC EMERGENCY

External Pacing

Unstable Bradycardia

!
INDICATIONS
  • Symptomatic Bradycardia unresponsive to drugs.
  • Mobitz II or CHB.
  • Sedation: Ketamine/Midazolam (Painful).
1
SETUP
  • Pads: Anterior-Posterior. Connect ECG.
  • Select "Pacer". Rate: 70-80 bpm.
2
CAPTURE (mA)
  • Start 30 mA. Increase gradually.
  • Look for Electrical Capture: Spike + Broad QRS.
  • Typical: 50-90 mA.
3
CONFIRMATION
  • Mechanical Capture: Palpate Femoral Pulse.
  • Set current 10 mA above threshold.
Reference: Resus Council UK ALS 2021.
CARDIAC EMERGENCY

Pericardiocentesis

Tamponade Decompression

!
DIAGNOSIS
  • Beck's Triad. POCUS: Fluid + RV Collapse.
  • Note: Use Thoracotomy for Traumatic Arrest.
1
APPROACH
  • Sub-Xiphoid: 1cm inferior to Left Xiphocostal Angle.
  • Aim for Left Shoulder. Angle 15-30°.
2
TECHNIQUE
  • US Guided: Visualize needle.
  • ECG Guided: V1 clip. ST Elevation = Contact.
  • Advance slowly while aspirating.
3
MANAGEMENT
  • Aspirate fluid. Leave pigtail if possible.
  • Monitor.
Reference: ESC Guidelines 2015 / RCEM Guidance.
