Hysterotomy Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Maternal Cardiac Arrest.
‚Ä¢ Uterus ‚â• Umbilicus (~20wks).
‚Ä¢ No ROSC at 4 mins.
‚Ä¢ GOAL: Delivery by minute 5.
Equipment
Scalpel (Size 10/20) - KEY
Trauma Shears (Essential for speed)
Large Abdominal Swabs (x5)
Chlorhexidine (Splash only)
1-0 Absorbable Suture (Vicryl)
Needle Holder
Oxytocin (10 IU) & TXA 1g
Neonatal Resus Kit
Midline incision from Xiphisternum to Symphysis Pubis
Detailed Steps
  1. Location: Operate on the trolley. DO NOT move to theatre. Maintain CPR/displacement.
  2. Incision: Midline Vertical. From Xiphisternum to Pubis. Go Deep - cut through skin and fat in one swipe.
  3. Exposure: Separate rectus muscles manually (rip apart). Identify peritoneum.
  4. Bladder: Identify bladder reflection. Push/Retract downwards.
  5. Uterus: Small vertical cut (3-4cm) in lower segment (less vascular).
  6. Extend: Insert two fingers to lift uterine wall off baby. Use Shears to extend cut upwards.
  7. Deliver: Head out. Suction mouth. Clamp cord. Handover to Paeds.
OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY

Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Perimortem C-Section (>20 Weeks)

  • GOAL: DELIVERY < 5 MINS
  • Indication: Maternal Arrest. Uterus ‚â• Umbilicus.
  • Location: Operate on trolley. DO NOT move to theatre.
1
CPR MODIFICATIONS
  • Manual Uterine Displacement to LEFT (Relieve IVC).
  • Splash Chlorhexidine. Prepare Shears.
  • Continue CPR during prep.
2
THE INCISION
  • Vertical Midline: Xiphisternum to Pubis.
  • Incise deep through skin/fat. Separate rectus muscles bluntly.
  • Tip: Don't worry about bleeding; no cardiac output.
3
UTERINE ENTRY
  • Reflect bladder downwards.
  • Make small vertical incision in lower uterus.
  • Insert fingers to protect baby. Extend up with Shears.
4
POST-DELIVERY
  • Clamp & Cut Cord. Handover baby.
  • Manual Placenta removal. Pack uterus with swabs.
  • Drugs: Oxytocin 10iu IV, Antibiotics, TXA 1g.
  • Closure: Mass closure if ROSC achieved.
Reference: Resuscitation Council UK (2025 Update).
Breech Walkthrough
Principles
‚Ä¢ Hands Off: Touching causes gasping/head extension.
‚Ä¢ Gravity is the main force. Let the breech hang.
‚Ä¢ Only intervene if stuck (Time limits apply).
‚Ä¢ Call Senior Obstetrician & Paeds immediately.
Pinard's: Flex knee by abducting thigh
Manoeuvres (If descent stops)
  1. LEGS (Pinard's):
    - Insert finger into popliteal fossa (behind knee).
    - Abduct leg laterally (away from body).
    - This causes flexion of the knee to clear the canal.
  2. ARMS (Loveset's):
    - Hold baby by bony pelvis (sacrum/hips).
    - Rotate body 180¬∞. Friction sweeps posterior arm down across face.
    - Rotate back 180¬∞ for the other arm.
  3. HEAD (MSV):
    - Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit
    - Lay baby on your forearm (straddle).
    - Middle finger in mouth (on jaw) to FLEX head.
    - Other hand on shoulders for traction.
OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY

Emergency Breech

Vaginal Delivery Manoeuvres

  • THE GOLDEN RULE: HANDS OFF.
  • Allow breech to hang by gravity.
  • Keep room warm. Note time of buttock delivery.
1
LEGS STUCK (Pinard's)
  • Indication: Legs extended up (Frank Breech).
  • Finger in popliteal fossa.
  • Flex knee laterally (abduct). Leg will flex out.
2
ARMS STUCK (Loveset's)
  • Sign: Winged Scapula visible.
  • Hold bony pelvis. Rotate 180¬∞. Arm sweeps down over chest.
  • Rotate back 180¬∞ for second arm.
3
HEAD STUCK (MSV)
  • Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit:
  • Baby on forearm. Finger in mouth. Flex head. Lift up.
  • Tip: Assistant applies Suprapubic Pressure.

Thoracotomy Walkthrough
Indications (FPHC/RCEM)
‚Ä¢ Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins. Signs of Life on scene.
‚Ä¢ Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins. Witnessed. Signs of Life.
‚Ä¢ "Signs of Life": Pupils, gasping, cardiac motion.
Equipment
Scalpel (Size 20 preferred)
Heavy Scissors (Mayo/Tuff Cut)
Gigli Saw or Bone Cutters
Finochietto Retractor (Rib Spreader)
Toothed Forceps
Vascular Clamp (Satinsky)
Skin Stapler
Clamshell Technique
  1. Intubate: Advance ETT to Right Main Bronchus if possible (deflate left lung).
  2. Incise: 5th ICS (Nipple line). Curvilinear from Mid-Axillary to Mid-Axillary (Bow tie).
  3. Sternum: Tunnel under sternum with fingers. Cut transversely with Gigli/Shears.
  4. Open: Lift chest wall ("Clamshell"). Insert Retractor.
  5. Pericardium: Pick up with toothed forceps. Longitudinal cut (Sup to Inf).
    Warning: Phrenic Nerve runs laterally!
  6. Deliver Heart: Two hands. Clap hands. Squeeze apex to base.
TRAUMA ARREST

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

"The Clamshell" Approach

  • Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins. Signs of Life.
  • Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins. Witnessed.
  • Consider: Is this futile? (Asystole > 20 mins).
1
INCISION (CLAMSHELL)
  • Stop CPR briefly. 5th Intercostal Space.
  • Incise: Mid-Axillary Line to Mid-Axillary Line.
  • Go Deep: Cut through intercostal muscles.
2
EXPOSURE
  • Cut Sternum with Gigli saw or Shears.
  • Lift chest wall. Open Pericardium (Longitudinal).
  • Avoid Phrenic Nerve (Runs on lateral pericardium).
3
ACTIONS (H.O.T.)
  • Heart: Evacuate tamponade. Internal Massage (Two hands).
  • Outflow: Plug holes (Foley/Finger/Staples).
  • Thorax: Clamp Aorta (Descending).
4
AORTIC CROSS CLAMP
  • Retract left lung medially. Identify Descending Aorta on spine.
  • Apply vascular clamp.
  • Alternatively: Compress aorta against spine with fist.
Reference: FPHC & RCEM Consensus Statement on Traumatic Cardiac Arrest (2025).
Escharotomy Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Circumferential full-thickness burns.
‚Ä¢ Chest: Ventilation difficulty / High Peak Pressures.
‚Ä¢ Limbs: Ischaemia (Pallor, Absent Doppler, Pain on passive stretch).
Equipment
Diathermy (Preferred - reduces bleeding)
Scalpel (Size 10/20) - if no diathermy
Marker pen (Draw lines first)
Kaltostat/Algisite Dressings
Lines of Incision
  1. Chest: "Roman Breastplate". Vertical lines in Anterior Axillary lines. Connect top (clavicle) & bottom (costal margin).
  2. Limbs: Mid-Lateral and Mid-Medial lines.
  3. Hands: Mid-lateral fingers. Dorsal hand incisions.
  4. Depth: Cut through eschar to subcut fat. Edges must gape widely.
  5. Caution:
    - Medial Elbow (Ulnar Nerve).
    - Fibular Head (Common Peroneal Nerve).
    - Flexor Retinaculum (Median Nerve).
BURNS EMERGENCY

Escharotomy

Circumferential Burns

  • Chest: Ventilation compromise.
  • Limbs: Ischaemia (Pallor, Absent Doppler).
  • Procedure is painless in full-thickness burns (nerves destroyed).
1
CHEST ("BREASTPLATE")
  • Vertical: Bilateral Anterior Axillary Lines.
  • Transverse: Connect tops (Clavicle) & bottoms (Costal Margin).
  • Ensure chest wall releases/springs open.
2
UPPER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial: Shoulder to wrist.
  • Hand: Dorsal incisions over metacarpals. Mid-lateral fingers.
  • Avoid Ulnar Nerve: Pass ANTERIOR to Medial Epicondyle.
3
LOWER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial: Hip to ankle.
  • Avoid Common Peroneal: Pass POSTERIOR to Fibular Head.
Reference: LSEBN Guidelines (2025).
Chest Drain Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Traumatic Haemothorax / Pneumothorax.
‚Ä¢ Massive Pleural Effusion (Unstable).
Equipment
Chlorhexidine
Lidocaine 1% (Skin -> Periosteum -> Pleura)
Scalpel
Kelly Forceps (Curved)
Chest Tube (28-32F for Trauma)
1-0 Silk Suture
Seal Bottle (Filled with water)
Lat Dorsi (Post), Pec Major (Ant), Axilla (Sup), Nipple (Inf)
Technique
  1. Site: "Triangle of Safety". 5th ICS, Anterior Axillary Line.
  2. LA: Infiltrate skin, intercostal muscles, and pleura (aspirate air/blood).
  3. Incision: 3cm transverse cut parallel to rib.
  4. Dissect: Bluntly with Kellys over upper border of lower rib (Avoid NV bundle).
  5. Breach: Push forceps through pleura ("Pop"). Open jaws to spread.
  6. Sweep: Finger sweep 360¬∞ to clear adhesions/clots.
  7. Insert: Tube Apical (Air) or Posterior (Fluid). Connect to seal.
THORACIC EMERGENCY

Chest Drain (Open)

Trauma / Haemothorax

1
SAFETY TRIANGLE
  • Lat Dorsi (Posterior). Pec Major (Anterior). Axilla (Superior).
  • Target: 5th ICS, Anterior Axillary Line.
2
INCISION & DISSECTION
  • Local Anaesthetic. 3cm Transverse incision.
  • Blunt dissection with Kellys.
  • Stay on Upper Border of Lower Rib to avoid Neurovascular Bundle.
3
PLEURAL ENTRY
  • Push forceps through pleura ("Pop").
  • Open forceps widely to create track.
  • Finger Sweep is mandatory (verify you are in chest, not abdomen).
4
INSERTION
  • Insert Large Bore Tube (28-32F).
  • Look for "Fogging" and "Swinging" in tube.
  • Connect to seal. Suture (Mattress or Stay). Tape. CXR.

Pericardio Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Cardiac Tamponade (Non-Traumatic).
‚Ä¢ Beck's Triad + US confirmation.
‚Ä¢ Note: In Traumatic Tamponade, Thoracotomy is preferred due to clotted blood.
Equipment
Spinal Needle / Long Cannula (18G)
20ml Syringe + 3-way Tap
Ultrasound (Probe: Phased Array)
ECG Clip (V1) if doing blind
Collection bag
Technique (Sub-Xiphoid)
  1. Site: 1cm below Left Xiphocostal Angle.
  2. Angle: Shallow! 15-30 degrees to skin.
  3. Aim: Towards Left Shoulder (Mid-clavicle).
  4. Advance: Aspirate constantly.
    - US Guided (Best): Watch needle enter fluid.
    - ECG Guided: Clip V1 to needle. ST Elevation = Contact with myocardium (Pull back!).
  5. Fluid: Aspirate blood/fluid. Monitor BP.
  6. Verify: Fluid should NOT clot (pericardial blood is defibrinated).
CARDIAC EMERGENCY

Pericardiocentesis

Tamponade Decompression

  • Beck's Triad (Hypotension, Muffled Sounds, JVP).
  • POCUS: Fluid + RV Diastolic Collapse.
  • In Trauma -> Consider Thoracotomy.
1
APPROACH
  • Sub-Xiphoid: 1cm inferior to Left Xiphocostal Angle.
  • Aim for Left Shoulder. Angle 15-30¬∞.
  • Tip: Going too steep hits the Liver/Stomach. Go flat.
2
TECHNIQUE
  • US Guided: Visualize needle entering fluid pocket.
  • Agitation Study: Inject 1ml agitated saline to confirm location.
  • Advance slowly while aspirating.
3
MANAGEMENT
  • Aspirate fluid until hemodynamics improve.
  • If bloody: Drop on gauze. Clots = Intracardiac. No Clots = Pericardial.
  • Leave pigtail drain if possible.
Reference: ESC Guidelines 2015 / RCEM Guidance.
Pacing Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Symptomatic Bradycardia unresponsive to drugs (Atropine/Adrenaline).
‚Ä¢ Mobitz II or Complete Heart Block (Risk of Asystole).
Equipment
Defib/Pacer Unit (Zoll/Philips)
Pacing Pads (Multifunction)
ECG Electrodes (3-lead) - Essential
Sedation (Ketamine/Midaz) - Essential
Best placement: Anterior (Apex) + Posterior (Between Scapulae)
Steps
  1. Pads: Place Anterior-Posterior (Sandwich the heart). Connect 3-Lead ECG.
  2. Mode: Select "Pacer" on Defib.
  3. Rate: Set Demand Rate to 70-80 bpm.
  4. Current (mA): Start low (30mA). Increase gradually.
  5. Capture (Electrical): Look for vertical spike followed immediately by broad QRS complex.
  6. Capture (Mechanical): MUST PALPATE PULSE. (Femoral).
  7. Safety: Identify threshold, then set +10mA above.
CARDIAC EMERGENCY

External Pacing

Unstable Bradycardia

  • Symptomatic Bradycardia unresponsive to drugs.
  • Sedation: Ketamine/Midazolam. Pacing is extremely painful (skeletal muscle contraction).
1
SETUP
  • Pads: Anterior-Posterior is superior to Anterior-Lateral.
  • Ensure 3-Lead ECG is connected (Pacer needs to sense R waves).
  • Select "Pacer". Rate: 70-80 bpm.
2
CAPTURE (mA)
  • Start 30 mA. Increase by 10 mA steps.
  • Look for Electrical Capture: Pacer Spike -> Broad QRS.
  • Note: Skeletal muscle twitching ‚â† Capture. Look at the ECG.
3
CONFIRMATION
  • Mechanical Capture: Palpate Femoral Pulse.
  • Do not use Carotid (muscle twitching mimics pulse).
  • Set current 10 mA above capture threshold (Safety Margin).
Reference: Resus Council UK ALS 2021.
Subclavian Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Crash vascular access.
‚Ä¢ IJ/Femoral not suitable/accessible.
‚Ä¢ High success rate in hypovolemia (Vein held open by fibrous tissue).
Equipment
CVC Kit (Triple Lumen)
Sterile Gown/Gloves/Drapes
Saline Flush x3
Suture (Silk/Prolene)
Scalpel
Landmarks
  1. Position: Head down (Trendelenburg) to distend vein. Rolled towel vertically between shoulder blades (retracts clavicle).
  2. Landmark: Junction of Medial/Middle third of clavicle.
  3. Insert: 1cm inferior to clavicle.

VASCULAR ACCESS

Subclavian Line

Infraclavicular Landmark Approach

  • Use Ultrasound if available. Crash use only.
  • Right side preferred (Lower dome of pleura = less pneumothorax risk).
1
POSITION
  • Trendelenburg 15¬∞. Rolled towel between scapulae.
  • Head turned away.
2
LANDMARKS
  • Junction of Medial/Middle third of clavicle.
  • Insert 1cm below. Aim: Sternal Notch.
  • Keep flat: Needle must be parallel to floor to avoid lung.

Sengstaken Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Life-threatening Variceal Haemorrhage.
‚Ä¢ Uncontrolled by endoscopy/drugs.
‚Ä¢ Intubate First (RSI) to protect airway.
Equipment
Tube (Sengstaken/Minnesota)
50ml Syringe (Catheter tip)
Strong Clamps (x2) - Vital
Lubricant
1L Saline Bag (Weight for traction)
Technique
  1. Check: Inflate balloons underwater to check for leaks. Deflate fully.
  2. Insert: Lubricate well. Insert to 50cm mark. Aspirate gastric port (confirm stomach contents).
  3. Test Inflation: 50ml Air -> Gastric Balloon.
  4. X-Ray: CONFIRM position below diaphragm. DO NOT FULLY INFLATE UNTIL CONFIRMED.
  5. Full Inflation: Add 200ml Air (Total 250ml) -> Gastric Balloon. Clamp securely.
  6. Traction: Pull back until resistance. Hang 1kg weight (1L Saline) over IV pole pulley.
GI HAEMORRHAGE

Sengstaken / Minnesota

Uncontrollable Variceal Bleed

  • Intubate First. Mandatory.
  • Test balloons underwater. Lubricate well.
1
INSERTION & SAFETY
  • Insert to 50cm.
  • Inflate Gastric Balloon with 50ml Air ONLY.
  • STOP & CXR. Confirm balloon is below diaphragm.
  • If confirmed: Inflate to 250ml total. Clamp.
Warning: Inflating in oesophagus = Rupture/Death.
2
TRACTION
  • Gently pull back until resistance felt (Balloon hits GOJ).
  • Apply 1kg Traction (e.g. 1L Saline bag).
  • Hang bag over IV pole/bed end.
3
OESOPHAGEAL BALLOON
  • Rescue Only. Use only if bleeding continues despite traction.
  • Inflate to 30-40 mmHg (Use Manometer).
  • Deflate every 6-12h to prevent necrosis.
Reference: BSG Guidelines 2025.
Canthotomy Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Retrobulbar Haematoma (Orbital Compartment Syndrome).
‚Ä¢ Signs: Proptosis + Pain + Reduced Vision + Afferent Pupillary Defect.
‚Ä¢ IOP > 40 mmHg ("Rock hard" to palpation).
‚Ä¢ Time is Vision. No CT first. Cut then Scan.
Equipment
Lidocaine w/ Adrenaline (Reduce bleeding)
Syringe/Orange Needle
Saline (Irrigation)
Mosquito Forceps (Straight)
Iris/Tenotomy Scissors (Small sharp)
Toothed Forceps
Steps
  1. Inj: Inject 1-2ml LA into lateral canthus (Aim away from globe).
  2. Crush: Clamp Mosquito forceps horizontally across lateral canthus (crushes vessels). Hold 1 min.
  3. Canthotomy: Remove clamp. Cut the crushed skin horizontally (1cm).
  4. Cantholysis:
    - Pull lower lid down and away.
    - Palpate tight band: Inferior Crus of Lateral Canthal Tendon.
    - "Strum" it with scissors.
  5. Snip: Cut Inferior Crus (Infero-posterior direction). Lid should go loose.
OPHTHALMIC EMERGENCY

Lateral Canthotomy

Retrobulbar Haematoma

  • Proptosis + Trauma + Decreased VA.
  • IOP > 40 mmHg. Time is Vision (<120 mins).
1
PREPARE
  • Irrigate eye. Local Anaesthetic w/ Adrenaline.
  • Aim needle away from globe.
2
CRUSH & CUT
  • Crush lateral canthus with Mosquito Forceps (1 min).
  • Cut the crushed tissue (Skin & Orbicularis) horizontally.
3
CANTHOLYSIS (THE KEY STEP)
  • Pull lower lid down to reveal Inferior Crus.
  • "Strum" with scissors. Cut Inferior Crus.
  • Success: Lower lid hangs loose. IOP drops.
  • Re-check IOP. If still high -> Cut Superior Crus.
Reference: RCEM & Royal College of Ophthalmologists Consensus (2025).
Max-Fax Walkthrough
Priorities
‚Ä¢ Airway Obstruction (Blood/Teeth/Tongue).
‚Ä¢ Exsanguination (Midface/Scalp).
‚Ä¢ C-Spine protection.
‚Ä¢ Danger: Loss of airway landmarks.

Key Manoeuvres
  1. Sit Up: If conscious, lean forward. Gravity assists airway.
  2. Disimpaction (Le Fort):
    - Gloved fingers behind upper teeth/palate.
    - Forcefully pull midface forward.
    - Insert Bite Block to keep face forward (opens airway).
  3. Mandible: Jaw thrust. Suture tongue if flail (Heavy Silk).
  4. Nose: Rapid Rhinos or Foley (inflate, pull back, clamp).
  5. Scalp: Raney Clips or heavy sutures/staples.

Severe Max-Fax Bleed

Stabilisation

  • Conscious: Sit up, lean forward.
  • Unconscious: Intubate (RSI). Avoid nasal route.
  • Prepare for Front of Neck Airway (distorted anatomy).
1
MIDFACE REDUCTION
  • Action: Gloved fingers behind upper teeth.

2
NASAL PACKING

3
ADJUNCTS

LAST Walkthrough
Signs
‚Ä¢ Prodrome: Metallic taste, Perioral tingling, Tinnitus, Agitation.
‚Ä¢ CNS: Seizures, Coma.
‚Ä¢ CVS: Collapse, Bradycardia, Broad QRS, Asystole.
‚Ä¢ Warning: Propofol is NOT a substitute for Lipid.
Lipid Rescue Kit
Intralipid 20% (x3 bags)
50ml Syringe
Wide Bore Cannula
Giving Sets
Action
  1. Stop Injection immediately. Call Help.
  2. Airway: Intubate. Hyperventilate (Acidosis increases LA toxicity, aim pH > 7.45).
  3. Bolus 1: 1.5ml/kg Intralipid 20%.
    - 70kg Adult = 100ml.
  4. Infusion: 15ml/kg/hr.
    - 70kg Adult = 1000ml/hr.
  5. Repeat Bolus: If no stability after 5 mins, repeat bolus x2 max.
  6. CPR: Continue for >1 hour. Lipid takes time to "sink" the toxin.
ANAESTHETIC EMERGENCY

LAST (Lipid Rescue)

Local Anaesthetic Toxicity

  • CNS: Tingling, metallic taste, seizure.
  • CVS: Bradycardia, Broad QRS, Arrest.
  • Action: STOP INJECTION. Call for Lipid.
1
AIRWAY & CPR
  • Intubate. Hyperventilate (alkalosis reduces free drug).
  • Control seizures (Benzo).
  • Prolonged CPR. Avoid Lidocaine (Class 1b antiarrhythmics).
  • Use Amiodarone. Reduce Adrenaline doses.
2
INTRALIPID 20% (BOLUS)
  • Bolus: 1.5 ml/kg (approx 100ml).
  • Give over 1 minute.
  • Repeat q5min if unstable (Max 3 boluses).
3
INTRALIPID 20% (INFUSION)
  • Rate: 15 ml/kg/hour (approx 1000ml/hr).
  • Double rate if unstable.
  • Continue for 10 mins after stability.
Reference: AAGBI Safety Guideline 2023.

Detailed Steps (DAS Plan D)
  1. Position: Maximize neck extension (Pillow under shoulders).
  2. Laryngeal Handshake:
    - Thumb & Middle finger grasp Hyoid.
    - Slide down to Thyroid Cartilage.
    - Index finger palpates Cricothyroid Membrane (CTM).
  3. Incision: Transverse stab through CTM. Blade edge facing YOU.
  4. Rotate: Rotate blade 90¬∞ (Sharp edge caudal). Do not withdraw blade.
  5. Bougie: Slide bougie down the flat side of blade into trachea.
  6. Railroad: Advance tube over bougie. Twist to pass skin.
  7. Ventilate: Inflate cuff, check EtCO2.
DAS GUIDELINES 2025

Surgical Airway (eFONA)

Can't Intubate, Can't Oxygenate (CICO)

‚ö†Ô∏è DECLARE "CICO". OPEN AIRWAY TROLLEY.
1
DECLARE & PREPARE
  • Call out: "CICO. Proceeding to Emergency Front of Neck Airway."
  • Action: 100% Oxygen to face. Extend Neck maximally.
  • Identify CTM (Laryngeal Handshake).
2
INCISION (SCALPEL)
  • Transverse Stab through CTM.
  • Turn Blade 90¬∞ (Sharp edge caudal/towards feet).
  • DO NOT REMOVE SCALPEL. Swap hands if needed.
3
RAILROAD (BOUGIE-TUBE)
  • Slide Bougie down the blade into trachea.
  • Remove Scalpel. Railroad 6.0 ETT.
  • Twist tube to pass vocal cords. Inflate cuff.
4
CONFIRMATION
  • Connect BVM. Confirm EtCO2 (Gold Standard).
  • Suction trachea. Secure tube. Sedate.
Reference: Difficult Airway Society (DAS) 2025 Guidelines.
