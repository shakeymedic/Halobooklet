How to use this guide
1. The Two-Page System
Every procedure consists of two sequential pages:

Page A: The Walkthrough (Preparation)
Use this during the "Kit Dump" phase. It includes specific equipment checklists, anatomy notes, and detailed setup instructions.

Page B: The Flowchart (Action)
Use this during the procedure itself. Designed for high-stress cognitive processing: high contrast, white space, and clear steps.
2. Design Principles
Standard Steps (Grey): Routine actions and setups.
Critical Steps (Red): Dangerous actions (incisions) or stops.
White Space: Blocks are separated to prevent reading errors.

Detailed Steps
  1. Location: Operate on trolley. Maintain CPR.
  2. Incision: Midline Vertical. Xiphisternum to Pubis. Go Deep.
  3. Uterus: Small vertical cut lower segment.
  4. Extend: Fingers in to protect baby face. Use Shears to extend up.
  5. Deliver: Head out. Clamp cord. Handover.
  6. Placenta: Manual removal. Pack uterus.
OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY

Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Perimortem C-Section (>20 Weeks)

  • GOAL: DELIVERY < 5 MINS
  • Indication: Maternal Arrest. Uterus ‚â• Umbilicus.
  • Location: Operate on trolley. DO NOT move to theatre.
1
CPR MODIFICATIONS
  • Manual Uterine Displacement to LEFT.
  • Splash Chlorhexidine. Prepare Shears.
2
THE INCISION
  • Vertical Midline: Xiphisternum to Pubis.
  • Incise deep through skin/fat. Separate muscles.
3
UTERINE ENTRY
  • Vertical incision in uterus. Lift wall away.
  • Extend with Shears. Deliver Baby.
4
POST-DELIVERY
  • Manual Placenta removal. Close uterus.
  • Drugs: Oxytocin 10iu, Abx, TXA 1g.
Reference: Resuscitation Council UK (2025 Update).

Principles

Manoeuvres

  2. Arms (Loveset's): Hold bony pelvis. Rotate 180¬∞. Arm sweeps down. Rotate back 180¬∞.
  3. Head (MSV): Baby on forearm. Finger in mouth (flex jaw). Hand on shoulders. Flex head. Lift up.

Emergency Breech

Vaginal Delivery Manoeuvres

  • THE GOLDEN RULE: HANDS OFF.
  • Allow breech to hang. Call Obs/Paeds.
  • Only intervene if descent stops.
1
LEGS STUCK (Pinard's)
  • Finger in popliteal fossa.
  • Flex knee laterally (abduct). Leg will flex out.
2
ARMS STUCK (Loveset's)
  • Signs: Winged Scapula.
  • Hold bony pelvis. Rotate 180¬∞. Arm sweeps down.
3
HEAD STUCK (MSV)
  • Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit:
  • Baby on forearm. Finger in mouth. Flex head. Lift up.

Thoracotomy Walkthrough
Indications (FPHC/RCEM)
‚Ä¢ Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins. Signs of Life on scene.
‚Ä¢ Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins. Witnessed. Signs of Life.
‚Ä¢ "Signs of Life": Pupils, gasping, cardiac motion.
Equipment
Scalpel (Size 10/20)
Heavy Scissors (Mayo)
Gigli Saw / Shears
Finochietto Retractor
Toothed Forceps
Vascular Clamp
Skin Stapler
Clamshell Technique
  1. Intubate: Right Main Bronchus if possible.
  2. Incise: 5th ICS. Mid-Ax to Mid-Ax (Bow tie).
  3. Sternum: Cut transversely with Gigli/Shears.
  4. Open: Lift chest wall. Retract.
  5. Pericardium: Longitudinal cut (Anterior to Phrenic).
  6. Actions: Deliver heart. Squeeze. Plug holes. Clamp Aorta.
TRAUMA ARREST

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

"The Clamshell" Approach

  • Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins. Signs of Life.
  • Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins. Witnessed.
1
INCISION (CLAMSHELL)
  • Stop CPR briefly. 5th Intercostal Space.
  • Incise: Mid-Axillary Line to Mid-Axillary Line.
2
EXPOSURE
  • Cut Sternum with Gigli saw or Shears.
  • Lift chest wall. Open Pericardium (Longitudinal).
3
ACTIONS
  • Evacuate clots. Deliver heart.
  • Control Bleeding: Finger / Staples / Foley.
4
AORTIC CROSS CLAMP
  • Retract left lung medially. Identify Aorta.
  • Apply vascular clamp.
Reference: FPHC & RCEM Consensus Statement on Traumatic Cardiac Arrest (2025).
Escharotomy Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Circumferential full-thickness burns.
‚Ä¢ Chest: Ventilation difficulty.
‚Ä¢ Limbs: Ischaemia (Pallor, Absent Doppler).
Equipment
Diathermy (Preferred)
Scalpel (Size 10/20)
Marker pen
Kaltostat/Algisite
Lines of Incision
  1. Chest: "Roman Breastplate". Vertical Anterior Axillary lines. Connect top/bottom.
  2. Limbs: Mid-Lateral and Mid-Medial.
  3. Depth: Cut through eschar to subcut fat. Edges must gape.
  4. Caution: Avoid Ulnar nerve (Medial elbow) & Common Peroneal (Fibular head).
BURNS EMERGENCY

Escharotomy

Circumferential Burns

  • Chest: Ventilation compromise.
  • Limbs: Ischaemia (Pallor, Absent Doppler).
1
CHEST ("BREASTPLATE")
  • Vertical: Bilateral Anterior Axillary Lines.
  • Transverse: Connect tops & bottoms.
2
UPPER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial: Shoulder to wrist.
  • Caution: Avoid Ulnar Nerve at medial elbow.
3
LOWER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial: Hip to ankle.
  • Caution: Avoid Common Peroneal at Fibular Head.
Reference: LSEBN Guidelines (2025).

  2. Incision: 3cm transverse.

  4. Breach: Forceps through pleura ("Pop").
  5. Sweep: Finger sweep 360¬∞.
  6. Insert: Tube Apical/Posterior.
THORACIC EMERGENCY

Chest Drain (Open)

Trauma / Haemothorax

1
SAFETY TRIANGLE
  • Lat Dorsi. Pec Major. Axilla.
  • Target: 5th ICS, Anterior Axillary Line.
2
INCISION & DISSECTION
  • Local Anaesthetic. 3cm Transverse incision.
  • Blunt dissection. Stay on Upper Border of Rib.
3
PLEURAL ENTRY
  • Push forceps through pleura ("Pop").
  • Open forceps widely. Finger Sweep.
4
INSERTION
  • Insert Large Bore Tube (28-32F).
  • Connect to seal. Suture. Tape. CXR.
Reference: BTS & RCEM Guidelines (2025).
Pericardio Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Cardiac Tamponade (Non-Traumatic).
‚Ä¢ Beck's Triad + US confirmation.
‚Ä¢ In Trauma: Thoracotomy is preferred.
Equipment
Spinal Needle / Long Cannula
20ml Syringe
Ultrasound
ECG Clip (V1) if blind
Technique
  1. Site: 1cm below Left Xiphocostal Angle.
  2. Angle: 15-30 degrees to skin.
  3. Aim: Towards Left Shoulder.
  4. Advance: Aspirate constantly. Watch US/ECG.
  5. Fluid: Aspirate blood/fluid. Monitor BP.
CARDIAC EMERGENCY

Pericardiocentesis

Tamponade Decompression

  • Beck's Triad. POCUS: Fluid + RV Collapse.
  • In Trauma -> Consider Thoracotomy.
1
APPROACH
  • Sub-Xiphoid: 1cm inferior to Left Xiphocostal Angle.
  • Aim for Left Shoulder. Angle 15-30¬∞.
2
TECHNIQUE
  • US Guided: Visualize needle.
  • ECG Guided: V1 clip. ST Elevation = Contact.
  • Advance slowly while aspirating.
3
MANAGEMENT
  • Aspirate fluid. Leave pigtail if possible.
  • Monitor.
Reference: ESC Guidelines 2015 / RCEM Guidance.
