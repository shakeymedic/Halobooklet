Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Call for Help & Roles
‚Ä¢ Call 2222 "Maternal Cardiac Arrest".
‚Ä¢ Declare Major Haemorrhage Protocol.
‚Ä¢ Assign Roles:
1. Person to manage Major Haemorrhage (blood/transfusion).
2. Person to lead ALS (Defib/Drugs).
3. Neonatal Team/Person for NLS (Baby).
‚Ä¢ Start ALS + Obstetric Modifications:
- Manually displace uterus to patient's LEFT.
- Intubate early (smaller ETT e.g., 6.0/7.0).
- IV access x2 (Above Diaphragm).
Equipment & Setup
Scalpel: Size 10 or 20
Tuff Cut Scissors
Chlorhexidine (Splash)
Suction x2 (Wide bore/Yankauer)
Large Swabs (for packing)
Stapler / Clingfilm / Large Dressings
Post-Procedure Plan
If ROSC Achieved:
‚Ä¢ Mass closure of abdomen with large dressings, clingfilm or staples.
‚Ä¢ Immediate Transfer: Emergent Theatre with Obstetrics.
‚Ä¢ Continue ALS / Stabilisation during transfer.
Procedure Walkthrough
  1. Clean: Rapid splash with Chlorhexidine.
  2. Skin Incision: Vertical incision from Xiphisternum to Pubic Symphysis. Cut deep through skin and fat.
  3. Peritoneum: Bluntly enter peritoneum with fingers (tear it open) to avoid bowel injury.
  4. Uterus Exposure: Retract abdominal wall laterally.
  5. Uterine Incision: Vertical incision in the upper segment of uterus. Use scalpel to start, then scissors.
  6. Protect Baby: Insert two fingers to lift uterine wall away from fetal head. Extend cut with Tuff Cut Scissors.
  7. Delivery: Scoop head and shoulders. Do not pull by a single limb. Rotate if necessary.
  8. Placenta: One gentle tug with smooth traction. If not easy, leave in situ. Pack uterus.
Vertical Midline Incision: Speed is priority

Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Page 2: Action (Perimortem C-Section)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
  • GOAL: DELIVERY in less than 5 MINS
  • Indication: Maternal Arrest. Uterus greater than or equal to Umbilicus.
  • Call: 2222 Maternal Cardiac Arrest.
1
ABDOMINAL INCISION
  • Vertical Midline: Xiphisternum to Pubic Symphysis.
  • Incise deep through skin/fat. Separate rectus muscles bluntly.
  • Ignore bleeding; there is no cardiac output.
2
UTERINE INCISION
  • Enter peritoneum bluntly with fingers.
  • Vertical Incision in uterus (Avoids bladder/arteries).
  • Insert fingers to protect baby's face. Extend with Tuff Cut Scissors.
3
DELIVERY
  • Scoop head and shoulders. Do not pull on a single limb.
  • Rotate if necessary to free shoulders.
  • Clamp and Cut Cord. Handover to NLS Team.
4
POST-DELIVERY & CLOSURE
  • Placenta: One gentle tug. If stuck, leave in situ. One hand sweep if bleeding.
  • Drugs: Oxytocin 10iu IV, Antibiotics, TXA 1g.
  • Closure: Pack uterus. Close abdomen with dressings, clingfilm or staples.
  • Transfer: Emergent Theatre (Obstetrics) if ROSC.

Emergency Breech

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

Call & Principles
‚Ä¢ Call 2222 "Obstetric Emergency".
‚Ä¢ Position: Lithotomy. Buttocks to edge of bed.
‚Ä¢ Team: Obstetric Reg, Midwife, Neonatal Team, Anaesthetics.
‚Ä¢ HANDS OFF: Do NOT touch baby until umbilicus is visible.
‚Ä¢ Gravity: Allow baby to hang. Avoid traction.
‚Ä¢ Time: Deliver head within 5 mins of buttocks.
Specific Equipment
Forceps (Neville Barnes / Piper's)
Oxytocin (for post-delivery)
GTN Spray (Uterine relaxation)
Episiotomy Scissors
Resuscitaire (Heater On)
Post-Procedure Plan
‚Ä¢ Inspect for perineal tears.
‚Ä¢ Paediatric review for baby.
‚Ä¢ Transfer: Obstetric Unit / Theatre for repair.
Manoeuvres (If descent stops)
  1. Legs Stuck (Pinard's):
    - Insert fingers behind fetal knee (popliteal fossa).
    - Press to abduct thigh (move away from midline).
    - Leg will flex. Sweep it out.
  2. Arms Stuck (Loveset's):
    - Hold bony pelvis (thumbs on sacrum).
    - Rotate baby 180 degrees (Keep back up/anterior).
    - Posterior arm sweeps down over face. Deliver arm.
    - Rotate back 180 degrees to deliver second arm.
  3. Head Stuck (Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit):
    - Assistant: Suprapubic pressure (flexes head).
    - Operator: Rest baby on forearm ("Flying horse").
    - Fingers on cheekbones (malar). Flex head. Pull gently.
Pinard's: Abduct thigh to flex knee

Emergency Breech

Page 2: Action (Vaginal Manoeuvres)

  • THE GOLDEN RULE: HANDS OFF.
  • Allow breech to hang by gravity.
  • Keep room warm. Note time of buttock delivery.
1
LEGS STUCK (Pinard's)
  • Indication: Frank Breech (legs extended).
  • Finger in popliteal fossa.
  • Abduct thigh laterally. Knee flexes. Sweep out.
2
ARMS STUCK (Loveset's)
  • Sign: Winged Scapula visible.
  • Hold bony pelvis. Rotate 180 degrees. Arm sweeps over chest.
  • Rotate back 180 degrees for second arm.
3
HEAD STUCK (MSV)
  • Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit:
  • Baby on forearm. Fingers on cheekbones. Flex head.
  • Tip: Assistant applies Suprapubic Pressure.
4
POST-PROCEDURE
  • Inspect for perineal tears.
  • Paediatric review for baby (Check hips/limbs).
  • Active management of third stage (Oxytocin).

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Strict Indications
‚Ä¢ Call 2222 "Trauma Cardiac Arrest".
‚Ä¢ Penetrating: Arrest less than 15 mins.
‚Ä¢ Blunt: Arrest less than 10 mins (Highly selective).
‚Ä¢ Signs of Life: Pupils reacting, gasping, ECG activity, cardiac motion on US.
‚Ä¢ Goal: Release Tamponade, Internal Massage, Clamp Aorta.
Equipment Tray
Scalpel: Size 22 (Large blade)
Tuff Cut Scissors (Heavy Duty)
Gigli Saw (Handles + Wire) or Bone Cutters
Finochietto Retractor (Rib Spreader)
Toothed Forceps (Bonney's)
Vascular Clamp (Satinsky/DeBakey)
Internal Paddles (for Defib)
Artery Forceps (Spencer Wells/Kellys)
Foley Catheter (20F)
Prolene/Silk 2-0 (Horizontal Mattress)
Dressings (Large)
Post-Procedure Plan
If ROSC Achieved:
‚Ä¢ Close the chest with large dressings.
‚Ä¢ Immediate Transfer: Emergent Theatre with Cardiothoracics.
‚Ä¢ Sedate and Paralyse immediately.
Clamshell Technique
  1. PREP: Splash Betadine. Stop ventilation.
  2. INCISION (SKIN): 4th/5th ICS. Scalpel from Mid-Axillary to Mid-Axillary.
  3. INCISION (DEEP): Use Tuff Cut Scissors to cut through muscle and intercostals.
  4. STERNUM: Tunnel forceps under sternum. Cut sternum with Gigli Saw or heavy shears.
  5. RETRACT: Insert Finochietto. Handle points DOWN. Crank open.
  6. PERICARDIUM: Lift with toothed forceps. Cut longitudinally (Head to Toe).
  7. DELIVER HEART: Scoop heart out of sac ("Luxate"). Inspect for wounds.
  8. CONTROL: Internal massage. Clamp Aorta. Suture wounds (Horizontal Mattress).
Clamshell: Mid-axillary to Mid-axillary
üë∂ PAEDIATRIC NOTE
‚Ä¢ Indications are extremely rare.
‚Ä¢ Incision: Avoid cutting too low (Liver is large).
‚Ä¢ Defib: 4 Joules/kg (Internal 1-2 J/kg).

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

Page 2: Action ("The Clamshell")

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
  • Penetrating: Arrest less than 15 mins + Signs of Life.
  • Blunt: Arrest less than 10 mins + Witnessed.
  • Call: 2222 Trauma Cardiac Arrest.
1
INCISION ORDER
  • 1. Scalpel: Cut Skin and Subcut fat (Mid-Ax to Mid-Ax).
  • 2. Tuff Cut Scissors: Cut Muscle and Sternum.
  • Stop ventilation before cutting pleura.
2
ACTIONS (H.O.T.)
  • Heart: Relieve tamponade. Open Pericardium (Longitudinal).
  • Outflow: Plug holes (Foley/Finger).
  • Thorax: Clamp Descending Aorta against spine.
3
REPAIR (SUTURING)
  • Use Horizontal Mattress Sutures (Prolene/Silk).
  • Use Pledgets if available (to prevent tear through).
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è WARNING: DO NOT OCCLUDE CORONARY ARTERIES (LAD/CIRCUMFLEX). Lift vessels to suture under them if needed.
4
ROSC, CLOSURE & DEFIBRILLATION
  • Internal Defib: Sterile paddles directly on heart. 10-20 Joules.
  • No Internal Paddles?
    - Remove Finochietto Retractor.
    - Close Chest manually (push ribs together).
    - Defib externally (200J). Ensure no one touching.
  • ROSC? Clamp bleeding Internal Mammary Arteries with artery forceps.
üõë STOP CRITERIA
Asystole > 20 mins despite Tamponade release, Aortic Clamp and Fluid loading.
Consensus decision to cease.

Escharotomy

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications & Criteria
‚Ä¢ Call 2222 "Trauma Team" / "Anaesthetics".
‚Ä¢ Circumferential full-thickness burns (Painless, leathery, white/charred).
‚Ä¢ Chest: Ventilation difficulty. High peak pressures (greater than 30). Hypoxia.
‚Ä¢ Limbs: Ischaemia. Pain on passive stretch. Loss of pulses (Late sign).
Equipment
Diathermy (Ideal - cuts and coags)
Scalpel (Size 22) if no diathermy
Sharpie Marker (Draw lines first)
Kaltostat / Algisite (Haemostatic dressing)
Adrenaline 1:1000 (Soaked gauze)
Loose Bandages (Crepe)
Mark lines before cutting
Incision Technique
  1. MARK: Draw incision lines with marker. Follow anatomical safe lines.
  2. DEPTH: Cut through eschar (burned skin) down to subcutaneous fat.
    STOP: Do not cut deep fascia or muscle.
  3. RELEASE: Edges must gape widely. Run finger along cut to ensure no tight bands remain.
  4. CHEST ("Roman Breastplate"):
    - Vertical: Bilateral Anterior Axillary Lines.
    - Horizontal: Connect Clavicles and Costal Margins.
  5. UPPER LIMB:
    - Mid-Lateral and Mid-Medial lines (Shoulder to wrist).
    - Elbow Safety: Pass ANTERIOR to medial epicondyle (Avoid Ulnar Nerve).
  6. LOWER LIMB:
    - Mid-Lateral and Mid-Medial lines (Hip to ankle).
    - Knee Safety: Pass POSTERIOR to fibular head (Avoid Common Peroneal Nerve).
Post-Procedure Plan
‚Ä¢ Apply haemostatic alginate dressings. Loosely bandage.
‚Ä¢ Elevate limbs.
‚Ä¢ Transfer: Specialist Burns Centre / ICU.
üë∂ PAEDIATRIC NOTE
‚Ä¢ Hypothermia: Risk is extreme. Warm room.
‚Ä¢ Blood Loss: Relatively small loss causes shock.
‚Ä¢ Fluid: Parkland Formula (3-4ml x kg x %TBSA).

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY

LOWER LIMBS

  • Peroneal Nerve: Cut POSTERIOR to Fibular Head.
4

  • Elevate limbs.

Chest Drain (Open)

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications
‚Ä¢ Call 2222 "Trauma" or "Medical Emergency".
‚Ä¢ Trauma: Haemothorax, Pneumothorax (ventilated/unstable).
‚Ä¢ Medical: Empyema, Massive Effusion.
‚Ä¢ Tension Pneumothorax: Decompress with needle/finger FIRST if arresting.
‚Ä¢ Contraindications: Coagulopathy (Relative), Diaphragmatic Hernia.
Equipment
Chlorhexidine + Sterile Drape
Lidocaine 1% (20ml)
Scalpel (Size 10)
Spencer Wells Forceps (Straight)
Curved Kelly Forceps (Alternative)
Chest Tube: 28-32F (Trauma), 12-14F (Air)
Silk Suture (1-0)
Underwater Seal (Primed)
Dressings (Tegaderm/Mepore)
Triangle of Safety: Lat Dorsi, Pec Major, Axilla, Nipple
Open Technique
  1. POSITION: Supine. Arm abducted behind head ("Sunbathing"). Secure arm.
  2. LANDMARK: Safety Triangle. 5th Intercostal Space, Anterior Axillary Line.
  3. LOCAL: Infiltrate skin, muscle, and pleura. Aspirate fluid/air to confirm depth.
  4. INCISION: 3-4cm Transverse cut parallel to ribs.
  5. DISSECT: Use Straight Spencer Wells. Push closed, open to spread. Tunnel over the UPPER BORDER of the rib below (Avoid intercostal vessels).
  6. BREACH: Push forceps firmly through pleura ("POP"). Open jaws wide to dilate track.
  7. SWEEP: Insert finger. Rotate 360 degrees. Feel lung. Break adhesions. Confirm you are in chest, not abdomen.
  8. INSERT: Clamp tube tip with forceps. Guide into chest. Aim Apical (Air) or Basal (Blood). Connect seal. Secure.
Post-Procedure Plan
‚Ä¢ Connect to underwater seal.
‚Ä¢ Secure with sutures/dressings.
‚Ä¢ Request CXR to confirm position.
‚Ä¢ Monitor output.
üë∂ PAEDIATRIC NOTE
‚Ä¢ Tube Size: (Age/4) + 4.
‚Ä¢ Depth: Smaller depth. Don't go deep.
‚Ä¢ Finger: Use little finger for sweep.

  • Suture: Mattress or Stay suture (1-0 Silk). Tape securely.
  • Post-Procedure: Request CXR to confirm position. Monitor drain output.

Pericardiocentesis

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications
‚Ä¢ Call 2222 "Cardiac Arrest" or "Medical Emergency".
‚Ä¢ Non-Traumatic Tamponade: Viral, Malignancy, Uraemia.
‚Ä¢ Signs (Beck's): Hypotension, Distended Neck Veins, Muffled Sounds.
‚Ä¢ POCUS: Fluid + RV Diastolic Collapse.
‚Ä¢ Trauma Warning: Blood clots rapidly. Thoracotomy is superior.
Equipment
Ultrasound (Probe Cover + Gel)
Pericardiocentesis Kit OR
Central Line Kit (16G Grey Cannula)
20ml Syringe
3-Way Tap (Essential)
Agitated Saline (for verification)
Dressings
Agitated Saline (Verification)
1. Make: 9ml Saline + 1ml Air. Agitate between two syringes via 3-way tap until frothy.
2. Deliver: Inject rapidly via drain/needle while assistant watches USS.
3. Look for: Bubbles (Hyperechoic swirl) appearing in the pericardial fluid.
Echo-Guided Approach
Strategy: Find the largest pocket closest to skin (less than 1cm).
  1. Apical (Preferred):
    - 1-2cm Lateral to Apex Beat.
    - Aim towards Right Shoulder. Glide over rib.
    - Often safest route (avoids liver).
  2. Sub-Xiphoid (Traditional):
    - 1cm inferior to Left Xiphocostal angle.
    - Shallow angle (15 degrees). Aim towards Left Shoulder.
    - Longer path, passes near liver.
  3. Parasternal:
    - 5th Left ICS, close to sternum.
    - Insert Perpendicular.
    - Risk: Internal Thoracic Artery.
Post-Procedure Plan
‚Ä¢ Secure drain. CXR.
‚Ä¢ Urgent Cardiology/CT Surgery referral.
Use Ultrasound to select best window

Pericardiocentesis

Page 2: Action (Tamponade Decompression)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
  • Beck's Triad (Hypotension, Muffled Sounds, JVP).
  • POCUS: RV Diastolic Collapse = Tamponade.
  • In Traumatic Arrest use Thoracotomy.
  • Call: 2222 Cardiac Arrest.
1
APPROACH
  • Apical: Shortest path. Good seal.
  • Sub-Xiphoid: Shallow angle. Aim Left Shoulder.
  • Parasternal: Perpendicular. Watch for vessels.
2
EQUIPMENT HACK
  • No Kit? Use Central Line 16G (Grey) Cannula.
  • Attach 3-Way Tap.
  • Advantage: Leave plastic cannula in as drain.
3
EXECUTION & POST-PROCEDURE
  • Advance under US guidance. Aspirate continuously.
  • Verification: Inject agitated saline (Bubbles in pericardium?).
  • Fluid should NOT clot (defibrinated).
  • After: Secure drain. CXR. Urgent Cardiology/CT Surgery referral.

External Pacing

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

Indications
‚Ä¢ Call 2222 "Medical Emergency".
‚Ä¢ Unstable Bradycardia: less than 40 bpm + Shock/Syncope/Ischaemia.
‚Ä¢ Failed drug therapy (Atropine/Glyco).
‚Ä¢ Risk of Asystole: Mobitz II, Complete Heart Block, Pause greater than 3s.
Equipment
Defibrillator (Pacer Mode)
Pacing Pads (AP Position)
3-Lead ECG (Required for sensing)
Sedation (Ketamine/Midazolam)
Analgesia (Fentanyl)
Post-Procedure Plan
‚Ä¢ Cardiology Referral for temporary wire.
‚Ä¢ Monitor conscious level (Sedation).
Step-by-Step Configuration
  1. PADS: Apply Anterior-Posterior (Sandwich the heart).
  2. LEADS: Connect 3-lead ECG. Confirm R-wave markers on screen.
  3. MODE: Select PACER. Mode: Demand.
  4. RATE: Set to 70-80 bpm.
  5. CURRENT (mA): Start 30 mA. Increase by 10 mA.
  6. ELECTRICAL CAPTURE: Pacer Spike -> Broad QRS -> T wave.
  7. MECHANICAL CAPTURE: Palpate Femoral Pulse.
    WARNING: Skeletal muscle twitching is NOT capture. Carotid pulse is unreliable due to neck twitching.
  8. SETTING: Find threshold. Set final output to Threshold + 10 mA.
Anterior-Posterior Position is Best

External Pacing

Page 2: Action (Unstable Bradycardia)

  • Symptomatic Bradycardia unresponsive to drugs.
  • Sedation: Pacing is painful. Use Ketamine/Midaz.
  • Call: 2222 Medical Emergency.
1
SETUP
  • Pads: Anterior-Posterior preferred.
  • Connect 3-Lead ECG (Critical for sensing).
  • Select "Pacer". Rate: 70 bpm.
2
CAPTURE (mA)
  • Increase Current (mA) until capture seen.
  • Electrical Capture: Spike followed by Broad QRS.
  • Ignore skeletal muscle twitching. Look at ECG.
3
CONFIRMATION & POST-PROCEDURE
  • Mechanical Capture: Palpate Femoral Pulse.
  • Ensure pulse matches pacer rate.
  • Set output 10 mA above threshold.
  • After: Refer to Cardiology for Transvenous/Permanent Pacemaker. Monitor conscious level.
üõë PLAN B: NO CAPTURE?
‚Ä¢ Check Pads/Leads. Swap Pad position.
‚Ä¢ Start Adrenaline/Isoprenaline Infusion.
‚Ä¢ Consider Hyperkalaemia/Toxicity.

Subclavian Line

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications
‚Ä¢ Call 2222 "Medical Emergency" or "Trauma".
‚Ä¢ Hypovolaemic Shock (Vein remains patent).
‚Ä¢ C-Spine Immobilisation (IJ inaccessible).
‚Ä¢ Contraindications: Coagulopathy (Relative), Contralateral Pneumothorax.
Equipment
Central Line Kit (Triple Lumen)
Sterile Gown, Gloves, Drapes
Chlorhexidine
Lidocaine 1%
Saline Flushes x3
Suture (Silk) + Scalpel
Dressings (Tegaderm)
Post-Procedure Plan
‚Ä¢ CXR Mandatory.
‚Ä¢ Check line position.
‚Ä¢ Secure well.
Landmark Technique
  1. POSITION: 15 degree Trendelenburg. Rolled towel vertically between shoulder blades. Retract shoulders down.
  2. LANDMARK: Junction of Medial 1/3 and Middle 1/3 of Clavicle.
  3. LOCAL: Infiltrate skin and periosteum.
  4. INSERTION: 1cm inferior to clavicle junction. Bevel Up.
  5. AIM: Place finger in Sternal Notch. Aim towards it.
  6. TRAJECTORY: Keep needle PARALLEL TO FLOOR.
    - Hit clavicle.
    - "Walk" under the bone.
    - Aspirate constantly.
  7. ACCESS: Venous Flash -> Stabilise -> Wire (Must flow freely) -> Dilate -> Catheter.
  8. CHECK: CXR Mandatory (Check for Pneumothorax and Tip Position).
Landmark: Medial/Mid 1/3 Clavicle -> Sternal Notch

Subclavian Line

Page 2: Action (Infraclavicular Approach)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
  • Use Ultrasound if available. Landmark for crash only.
  • Right side preferred (Lower pleural dome).
  • Call: 2222 Medical Emergency.
1
POSITION
  • Trendelenburg 15 degrees. Towel between scapulae.
  • Shoulders retracted down. Head turned away.
2
LANDMARKS
  • Junction of Medial/Middle third of clavicle.
  • Insert 1cm below. Aim: Sternal Notch.
  • Keep flat: Parallel to floor (Avoids lung apex).
3
SELDINGER & POST-PROCEDURE
  • Flash -> Wire (must slide easily).
  • Dilate -> Catheter -> Aspirate all ports -> Suture.
  • Mandatory CXR. (Exclude Pneumothorax).
  • Check line position before use.
üõë PLAN B: FAILED ACCESS?
