Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Call for Help & Prepare
‚Ä¢ Call 2222 "Maternal Cardiac Arrest".
‚Ä¢ Declare Major Haemorrhage Protocol.
‚Ä¢ ED Consultant must attend.
‚Ä¢ Start ALS + Obstetric Modifications:
- Manually displace uterus to patient's LEFT.
- Intubate early (Use smaller ETT e.g., 6.0/7.0).
- IV access x2 (Above Diaphragm).
- Defib/Drugs as per standard ALS.
Equipment & Setup
Scalpel (Size 10 or 20 preferred)
Tuff Cut Shears
Chlorhexidine (Splash)
Suction x2 (Wide bore/Yankauer)
Large Swabs (for packing)
Neonatal Team & Resuscitaire Ready
Post-ROSC Check
1. Maternal ROSC?
If YES, treat reversible causes:
‚Ä¢ Hypovolaemia (Blood/TXA)
‚Ä¢ Eclampsia (Magnesium)
‚Ä¢ Embolism (Thrombolysis - consult first)
2. Debrief: Hot debrief immediately.
Procedure Walkthrough
  1. Clean: Rapid splash with Chlorhexidine.
  2. Skin Incision: Vertical incision from Xiphisternum to Pubic Symphysis. Cut deep through skin and fat.
  3. Peritoneum: Bluntly enter peritoneum with fingers (tear it open) to avoid bowel injury.
  4. Uterus Exposure: Retract abdominal wall laterally.
  5. Uterine Incision: Vertical incision in the upper segment of uterus. Use scalpel to start, then scissors.
  6. Protect Baby: Insert two fingers to lift uterine wall away from fetal head. Extend cut with shears.
  7. Delivery: Deliver head. Suction mouth/nose. Clamp and cut cord. Hand to Paeds.
  8. Placenta: Leave in situ unless easily detached. Pack uterus. Mass closure if ROSC.
Vertical Midline Incision: Speed is priority

Emergency Breech

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
General Principles
‚Ä¢ Position: Lithotomy. Buttocks to edge of bed.
‚Ä¢ Team: Obstetric Reg, Midwife, Neonatal Team, Anaesthetics.
‚Ä¢ HANDS OFF: Do NOT touch baby until umbilicus is visible.
‚Ä¢ Gravity: Allow baby to hang. Avoid traction.
‚Ä¢ Time: Deliver head within 5 mins of buttocks.
Specific Equipment
Forceps (Neville Barnes / Piper's)
Oxytocin (for post-delivery)
GTN Spray (Uterine relaxation)
Episiotomy Scissors
Resuscitaire (Heater On)
Pinard's: Abduct thigh to flex knee
Manoeuvres (If descent stops)
  1. Legs Stuck (Pinard's):
    - Insert fingers behind fetal knee (popliteal fossa).
    - Press to abduct thigh (move away from midline).
    - Leg will flex. Sweep it out.
  2. Arms Stuck (Loveset's):
    - Hold bony pelvis (thumbs on sacrum).
    - Rotate baby 180¬∞ (Keep back up/anterior).
    - Posterior arm sweeps down over face. Deliver arm.
    - Rotate back 180¬∞ to deliver second arm.
  3. Head Stuck (Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit):
    - Assistant: Suprapubic pressure (flexes head).
    - Operator: Rest baby on forearm ("Flying horse").
    - Fingers on cheekbones (malar). Flex head. Pull gently.

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Strict Indications
‚Ä¢ Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins.
‚Ä¢ Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins (Highly selective).
‚Ä¢ Signs of Life: Pupils reacting, gasping, ECG activity, cardiac motion on US.
‚Ä¢ Goal: Release Tamponade, Internal Massage, Clamp Aorta.
Equipment Tray
Scalpel: Size 22 (Large blade)
Tuff Cut Shears: Heavy Duty
Gigli Saw (Handles + Wire) or Bone Cutters
Finochietto Retractor (Rib Spreader)
Toothed Forceps (Bonney's)
Vascular Clamp (Satinsky/DeBakey)
Skin Stapler (Cardiac repair)
Foley Catheter (20F)
Clamshell: Mid-axillary to Mid-axillary
Clamshell Technique
  1. PREP: Splash Betadine. Stop ventilation (prevent lung injury).
  2. INCISION: 4th or 5th Intercostal Space (Nipple level). Cut from Mid-Axillary Line to Mid-Axillary Line. Curve under breasts.
  3. CUT MUSCLE: Cut deep through pectoralis/intercostals to pleura.
  4. CUT STERNUM: Tunnel forceps under sternum to clear pericardium. Use Gigli Saw or heavy shears to snap sternum.
  5. RETRACT: Insert Finochietto. Handle points DOWN (towards legs). Crank open.
  6. PERICARDIUM: Lift pericardium with toothed forceps. Cut longitudinally (Head to Toe). Avoid Phrenic Nerves laterally.
  7. DELIVER HEART: Scoop heart out of sac ("Luxate"). Inspect for wounds.
  8. CONTROL: Two-handed internal massage (clap hands). Staple wounds. Clamp Aorta against spine.

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

Page 2: Action ("The Clamshell")

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
  • Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins + Signs of Life.
  • Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins + Witnessed.
  • Futile: Asystole > 20 mins. Massive Head Injury.
1
INCISION (CLAMSHELL)
  • Stop ventilation briefly. 5th Intercostal Space.
  • Incise: Mid-Axillary to Mid-Axillary.
  • Go Deep: Cut through all muscle layers.
2
EXPOSURE
  • Fracture Sternum (Gigli saw / Shears).
  • Retract ribs. Open Pericardium (Longitudinal).
  • Avoid Phrenic Nerves (Lateral on pericardium).
3
ACTIONS (H.O.T.)
  • Heart: Relieve tamponade. Internal Massage (Clap).
  • Outflow: Plug holes (Foley/Finger/Staples).
  • Thorax: Clamp Descending Aorta.
4
AORTIC CROSS CLAMP
  • Retract left lung medially. Locate Aorta on spine.
  • Apply vascular clamp.
  • Alternative: Manual compression against spine.
Reference: FPHC & RCEM Consensus Statement (2025).

Escharotomy

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications & Criteria
‚Ä¢ Circumferential full-thickness burns (Painless, leathery, white/charred).
‚Ä¢ Chest: Ventilation difficulty. High peak pressures (>30). Hypoxia.
‚Ä¢ Limbs: Ischaemia. Pain on passive stretch. Loss of pulses (Late sign).
‚Ä¢ Note: Check Carboxyhaemoglobin (COHb).
Equipment
Diathermy (Ideal - cuts & coags)
Scalpel (Size 22) if no diathermy
Sharpie Marker (Draw lines first)
Kaltostat / Algisite (Haemostatic dressing)
Adrenaline 1:1000 (Soaked gauze)
Loose Bandages (Crepe)
Mark lines before cutting
Incision Technique
  1. MARK: Draw incision lines with marker. Follow anatomical safe lines.
  2. DEPTH: Cut through eschar (burned skin) down to subcutaneous fat.
    STOP: Do not cut deep fascia or muscle.
  3. RELEASE: Edges must gape widely. Run finger along cut to ensure no tight bands remain.
  4. CHEST ("Roman Breastplate"):
    - Vertical: Bilateral Anterior Axillary Lines.
    - Horizontal: Connect Clavicles and Costal Margins.
  5. UPPER LIMB:
    - Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial lines (Shoulder to wrist).
    - Elbow Safety: Pass ANTERIOR to medial epicondyle (Avoid Ulnar Nerve).
  6. LOWER LIMB:
    - Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial lines (Hip to ankle).
    - Knee Safety: Pass POSTERIOR to fibular head (Avoid Common Peroneal Nerve).

Escharotomy

Page 2: Action (Burns Release)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
  • Chest: Ventilatory compromise.
  • Limbs: Ischaemia (Pallor, Absent Doppler).
  • Procedure is painless in full-thickness burns.
1
CHEST ("BREASTPLATE")
  • Vertical: Bilateral Anterior Axillary Lines.
  • Transverse: Connect tops (Clavicle) & bottoms (Costal Margin).
  • Result: Chest wall must spring open.
2
UPPER LIMBS
  • Lines: Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial.
  • Hand: Dorsal incisions over metacarpals.
  • Ulnar Nerve: Cut ANTERIOR to Medial Epicondyle.
3
LOWER LIMBS
  • Lines: Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial.
  • Peroneal Nerve: Cut POSTERIOR to Fibular Head.
Reference: LSEBN Guidelines (2025).

Chest Drain (Open)

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications
‚Ä¢ Trauma: Haemothorax, Pneumothorax (ventilated/unstable).
‚Ä¢ Medical: Empyema, Massive Effusion.
‚Ä¢ Tension Pneumothorax: Decompress with needle/finger FIRST if arresting.
‚Ä¢ Contraindications: Coagulopathy (Relative), Diaphragmatic Hernia.
Equipment
Chlorhexidine + Sterile Drape
Lidocaine 1% (20ml)
Scalpel (Size 10)
Kelly Forceps (Large Curved)
Chest Tube: 28-32F (Trauma), 12-14F (Air)
Silk Suture (1-0)
Underwater Seal (Primed)
Triangle of Safety: Lat Dorsi, Pec Major, Axilla, Nipple
Open Technique
  1. POSITION: Supine. Arm abducted behind head ("Sunbathing"). Secure arm.
  2. LANDMARK: Safety Triangle. 5th Intercostal Space, Anterior Axillary Line.
  3. LOCAL: Infiltrate skin, muscle, and pleura. Aspirate fluid/air to confirm depth.
  4. INCISION: 3-4cm Transverse cut parallel to ribs.
  5. DISSECT: Use Kelly Forceps. Push closed, open to spread. Tunnel over the UPPER BORDER of the rib below (Avoid intercostal vessels).
  6. BREACH: Push forceps firmly through pleura ("POP"). Open jaws wide to dilate track.
  7. SWEEP: Insert finger. Rotate 360¬∞. Feel lung. Break adhesions. Confirm you are in chest, not abdomen.
  8. INSERT: Clamp tube tip with forceps. Guide into chest. Aim Apical (Air) or Basal (Blood). Connect seal. Secure.

Chest Drain (Open)

Page 2: Action (Trauma / Haemothorax)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
1
SAFETY TRIANGLE
  • Lat Dorsi (Posterior). Pec Major (Anterior). Axilla (Superior).
  • Target: 5th ICS, Anterior Axillary Line.
2
DISSECTION
  • Local Anaesthetic. 3cm Transverse incision.
  • Blunt dissection with Kellys.
  • Stay on Upper Border of Lower Rib (avoid vessels).
3
PLEURAL ENTRY
  • Breach pleura with forceps ("Pop"). Dilate track.
  • Finger Sweep mandatory (Verify cavity).
  • Insert Tube. Look for fogging/swinging.
4
SECURING
  • Connect to underwater seal.
  • Suture: Mattress or Stay suture (1-0 Silk).
  • Tape securely. Request CXR.

Pericardiocentesis

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications
‚Ä¢ Non-Traumatic Tamponade: Viral, Malignancy, Uraemia.
‚Ä¢ Signs (Beck's): Hypotension, Distended Neck Veins, Muffled Sounds.
‚Ä¢ POCUS: Fluid + RV Diastolic Collapse.
‚Ä¢ Trauma Warning: Blood clots rapidly. Thoracotomy is superior.
Equipment
Ultrasound (Probe Cover + Gel)
Pericardiocentesis Kit OR
Central Line Kit (16G Grey Cannula)
20ml Syringe
3-Way Tap (Essential)
Agitated Saline (for verification)
Echo-Guided Approach
Strategy: Find the largest pocket closest to skin (<1cm).
  1. Apical (Preferred):
    - 1-2cm Lateral to Apex Beat.
    - Aim towards Right Shoulder. Glide over rib.
    - Often safest route (avoids liver).
  2. Sub-Xiphoid (Traditional):
    - 1cm inferior to Left Xiphocostal angle.
    - Shallow angle (15¬∞). Aim towards Left Shoulder.
    - Longer path, passes near liver.
  3. Parasternal:
    - 5th Left ICS, close to sternum.
    - Insert Perpendicular.
    - Risk: Internal Thoracic Artery.
Use Ultrasound to select best window

Pericardiocentesis

Page 2: Action (Tamponade Decompression)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
  • Beck's Triad (Hypotension, Muffled Sounds, JVP).
  • POCUS: RV Diastolic Collapse = Tamponade.
  • In Traumatic Arrest -> Thoracotomy.
1
APPROACH
  • Apical: Shortest path. Good seal.
  • Sub-Xiphoid: Shallow angle. Aim Left Shoulder.
  • Parasternal: Perpendicular. Watch for vessels.
2
EQUIPMENT HACK
  • No Kit? Use Central Line 16G (Grey) Cannula.
  • Attach 3-Way Tap.
  • Advantage: Leave plastic cannula in as drain.
3

  • Verification: Inject agitated saline (Bubbles in pericardium?).
  • Fluid should NOT clot (defibrinated).
  • Drain -> Hemodynamics improve -> Secure.

External Pacing

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications
‚Ä¢ Unstable Bradycardia: < 40 bpm + Shock/Syncope/Ischaemia.
‚Ä¢ Failed drug therapy (Atropine/Glyco).
‚Ä¢ Risk of Asystole: Mobitz II, Complete Heart Block, Pause > 3s.
Equipment
Defibrillator (Pacer Mode)
Pacing Pads (AP Position)
3-Lead ECG (Required for sensing)
Sedation (Ketamine/Midazolam)
Analgesia (Fentanyl)
Anterior-Posterior Position is Best
Step-by-Step Configuration
  1. PADS: Apply Anterior-Posterior (Sandwich the heart).
  2. LEADS: Connect 3-lead ECG. Confirm R-wave markers on screen.
  3. MODE: Select PACER. Mode: Demand.
  4. RATE: Set to 70-80 bpm.
  5. CURRENT (mA): Start 30 mA. Increase by 10 mA.
  6. ELECTRICAL CAPTURE: Pacer Spike -> Broad QRS -> T wave.
  7. MECHANICAL CAPTURE: Palpate Femoral Pulse.
    WARNING: Skeletal muscle twitching is NOT capture. Carotid pulse is unreliable due to neck twitching.
  8. SETTING: Find threshold. Set final output to Threshold + 10 mA.

External Pacing

Page 2: Action (Unstable Bradycardia)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
  • Symptomatic Bradycardia unresponsive to drugs.
  • Sedation: Pacing is painful. Use Ketamine/Midaz.
1
SETUP
  • Pads: Anterior-Posterior preferred.
  • Connect 3-Lead ECG (Critical for sensing).
  • Select "Pacer". Rate: 70 bpm.
2
CAPTURE (mA)
  • Increase Current (mA) until capture seen.
  • Electrical Capture: Spike followed by Broad QRS.
  • Ignore skeletal muscle twitching. Look at ECG.
3
CONFIRMATION
  • Mechanical Capture: Palpate Femoral Pulse.
  • Ensure pulse matches pacer rate.
  • Set output 10 mA above threshold.

Sengstaken / Minnesota

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications
‚Ä¢ Exsanguinating Variceal Bleed (Oesophageal/Gastric).
‚Ä¢ Endoscopy unavailable or failed.
‚Ä¢ Prerequisite: Patient MUST be intubated (Airway protection).
‚Ä¢ Duration: Bridge to TIPS/Endoscopy (< 24 hrs).
Equipment
Sengstaken / Minnesota Tube
50ml Catheter Tip Syringe
Spigots / Clamps (x2)
Lubricant (Generous)
1L Saline Bag (Weight) + Giving Set
Manometer (for Oesophageal balloon)
Critical Safety Protocol
  1. TEST: Inflate balloons underwater. Check for leaks. Deflate fully.
  2. INSERT: Lubricate. Insert to 50cm mark at lips.
  3. ASPIRATE: Confirm gastric position (Acidic pH / aspirate).
  4. PRE-INFLATE: Inflate Gastric Balloon with 50ml Air ONLY.
  5. X-RAY CHECK: STOP. MANDATORY CXR. Confirm balloon is below diaphragm.
  6. FULL INFLATION: Inflate Gastric Balloon to 250ml total. Clamp.
  7. TRACTION: Pull back until resistance (Balloon hits GEJ). Attach 1kg weight (1L Saline) over pulley/pole.
  8. OESOPHAGEAL BALLOON: Rescue only. Inflate to 30-40mmHg if bleeding persists.
Gastric Balloon (Below Diaphragm) & Traction

Lateral Canthotomy

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Indications
‚Ä¢ Retrobulbar Haematoma: Orbital Compartment Syndrome.
‚Ä¢ Signs: Proptosis, Rock Hard Eye, APD, Vision Loss, 'Cherry Red' macula.
‚Ä¢ IOP: > 40 mmHg.
‚Ä¢ Rule: Do NOT wait for CT. Time is Vision (<120 mins).
Equipment
Lidocaine 1% or 2% with Adrenaline
2ml Syringe + Orange Needle
Saline Irrigation
Straight Mosquito Forceps
Iris/Tenotomy Scissors (Sharp)
Toothed Forceps (Adson)
Procedure Steps
  1. PREP: Irrigate. Inject LA into lateral canthus (Aim away from globe).
  2. CRUSH: Clamp Mosquito forceps horizontally across the lateral canthus (approx 1cm). Compress for 1 min (haemostasis).
  3. CANTHOTOMY (Skin): Remove forceps. Cut the crushed line horizontally to orbital rim.
  4. CANTHOLYSIS (Inferior Crus):
    - Pull lower eyelid down and out.
    - Palpate the tight "guitar string" band (Inferior Crus).
    - "Strum" it with scissors to identify.
    - CUT the Inferior Crus.
  5. CHECK: Lower lid should fall away. IOP should drop.
  6. STILL HIGH? Cut the Superior Crus (Upper lid band).
Cut Inferior Crus (Inferior Cantholysis)

Lateral Canthotomy

Page 2: Action (Retrobulbar Haematoma)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
  • Proptosis + Trauma + Decreased VA / APD.
  • IOP > 40 mmHg. Irreversible blindness in 2 hours.
1
PREPARE
  • Clean & Inject LA (w/ Adrenaline).
  • Protect globe during injection.
2
CRUSH & CUT
  • Crush lateral canthus (1 min).
  • Cut Skin & Orbicularis horizontally (Canthotomy).
3

  • Pull lower lid down. Identify Inferior Crus (Guitar String).
  • Cut Inferior Crus vertically.
  • Success: Lower lid hangs loose. IOP drops.
  • Refractory? Cut Superior Crus.

Severe Max-Fax Bleed

Page 1: Walkthrough (Preparation)

üß∞ HALO TROLLEY
Priorities
‚Ä¢ Airway: Distortion, loose teeth, blood, swelling.
‚Ä¢ Plan A: Conscious? Sit up + Suction.
‚Ä¢ Plan B: RSI. Anticipate difficult view. Have eFONA ready.
‚Ä¢ Bleeding: Maxillary artery is prolific. Scalp wounds bleed heavily.
‚Ä¢ C-Spine: Maintain precautions.
Equipment
Suction x2 (Wide bore)
Bite Blocks / McKesson Props
Rapid Rhino Packs (7.5cm / 9cm)
Foley Catheter (12-14F)
Heavy Suture (0 Silk) - Tongue Stitch
TXA 1g IV
Haemorrhage Control
  1. POSITION: "Lean and Spit" if conscious. Suction constantly.
  2. MIDFACE REDUCTION (Le Fort):
    - "Dish face" appearance.
    - Place gloved fingers behind upper teeth/palate.
    - PULL ANTERIORLY (Hard).
    - Disimpacts the maxilla. Stops bleeding.
    - Insert Bite Block to maintain position.
  3. TONGUE STITCH: Flail mandible? Stitch tongue tip and pull forward.
  4. NASAL PACKING:
    - Anterior: Rapid Rhino (Soak first).
    - Posterior: Foley. Inflate 15ml. Pull back against choanae. Clamp. Pack anteriorly.
