Do The Section (Procedure)
  1. Clean: Splash skin with chlorhexidine.
  2. Skin Incision: Vertical from Umbilicus to Pubic Symphysis.
    (Or Pfannenstiel if trained).
  3. Peritoneum: Make hole in peritoneum just below umbilicus with your fingers.
  4. Extend: Extend the hole with scissors (or scalpel).
  5. Uterus: Make uterus incision Vertically.

  7. Delivery: Extract fetus (may need fundal pressure).

Perimortem C-Section (>20 Weeks)

  • GOAL: DELIVERY < 5 MINS
  • Indication: Maternal Arrest. Uterus ‚â• Umbilicus.
  • Location: Operate on trolley. DO NOT move to theatre.
1
CPR MODIFICATIONS
  • Manual Uterine Displacement to LEFT (Relieve IVC).
  • Splash Chlorhexidine. Prepare Shears.

2
THE INCISION
  • Vertical Midline: Umbilicus to Pubic Symphysis.

  • Tip: Don't worry about bleeding; no cardiac output.
3
UTERINE ENTRY
  • Open peritoneum with fingers (below umbilicus).
  • Make vertical incision in uterus.
  • Insert fingers to protect baby. Extend up with Shears.
4
POST-DELIVERY

  • Manual Placenta removal (Gentle traction). Pack if stuck.
  • Drugs: Oxytocin 10iu IV, Antibiotics, TXA 1g.
  • Closure: Mass closure if ROSC achieved.

Breech Walkthrough
General Principles
‚Ä¢ Position: Lithotomy. Bring buttocks to edge of bed.
‚Ä¢ Team: Obstetric Reg, Midwife, Neonatal Team, Anaesthetics.
‚Ä¢ Hands Off: Do NOT touch baby until the umbilicus is visible (hairline for head).
‚Ä¢ Keep Back Up: Ensure fetal back remains anterior (facing ceiling).
‚Ä¢ Time: <5 mins from buttocks to head delivery.
Specific Equipment
Forceps (Neville Barnes or Wrigley's)
Oxytocin (for post-delivery)
GTN Spray (Uterine relaxation if trapped)
Episiotomy Scissors (Right-angled)
Resuscitaire (On and Heater warm)
Pinard's: Flex knee by abducting thigh
Manoeuvres (If descent stops)
  1. Legs Stuck (Pinard's):
    - Identify popliteal fossa (behind knee).
    - Press into fossa to abduct thigh (outwards).
    - This causes flexion. Sweep leg out.
  2. Arms Stuck (Loveset's):
    - Hold bony pelvis (thumbs on sacrum).
    - Rotate baby 180¬∞ (Keep back anterior/up).
    - Posterior arm sweeps down over face. Sweep it out.
    - Rotate back 180¬∞ to deliver second arm.
  3. Head Stuck (Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit):
    - Suprapubic pressure by assistant (flexes head).
    - Lay baby on your forearm (straddle).
    - Index/Middle finger on malar bones (cheek).
    - Other hand on shoulders. Flex head. Pull gently.

Emergency Breech

Vaginal Delivery Manoeuvres

  • THE GOLDEN RULE: HANDS OFF.
  • Allow breech to hang by gravity.
  • Keep room warm. Note time of buttock delivery.
1
LEGS STUCK (Pinard's)
  • Indication: Legs extended up (Frank Breech).
  • Finger in popliteal fossa.
  • Flex knee laterally (abduct). Leg will flex out.
2
ARMS STUCK (Loveset's)
  • Sign: Winged Scapula visible.
  • Hold bony pelvis. Rotate 180¬∞. Arm sweeps down over chest.
  • Rotate back 180¬∞ for second arm.
3
HEAD STUCK (MSV)
  • Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit:
  • Baby on forearm. Finger in mouth. Flex head. Lift up.
  • Tip: Assistant applies Suprapubic Pressure.

TRAUMA ARREST
Thoracotomy Walkthrough
Indications (Strict Criteria)
‚Ä¢ Penetrating (Stab/GSW): Arrest < 15 mins.
‚Ä¢ Blunt (Impact): Arrest < 10 mins (Strict selection).
‚Ä¢ Signs of Life: Pupillary response, Gasping, Cardiac motion on US, ECG activity.
‚Ä¢ Contraindications: Asystole > 20 mins, Massive head injury, Non-survivable injury.
‚Ä¢ Goal: Relieve Tamponade, Control Bleeding, Internal Massage, Cross-clamp Aorta.
Equipment Tray
Scalpel: Size 20 or 22 (Large blade)
Shears: Tuff Cut / Mayo (Heavy Duty)
Sternum Cutter: Gigli Saw (Handles + Wire) or Bone Cutters
Retractor: Finochietto (Rib Spreader)
Forceps: Toothed (Bonney's) to lift pericardium
Vascular Clamp: Satinsky or DeBakey (Large curved)
Skin Stapler: To close cardiac wounds
Foley Catheter: Size 20F (To plug holes)
Clamshell Incision: Mid-axillary to Mid-axillary
Clamshell Technique (Detailed)
  1. PREP: Splash Betadine (Don't scrub). Intubate right main bronchus if possible (Push tube 28cm) OR Stop ventilation briefly during incision.
  2. INCISION (Skin): Identify Sternal Notch and Xiphisternum. Go to 4th or 5th Intercostal Space (Nipple level men / Inframammary fold women). Cut from Mid-Axillary Line to Mid-Axillary Line (Curved "Bow Tie").
  3. MUSCLE CUT: Go DEEP. Cut through skin, fat, pectoralis, and intercostals in one sweep down to pleura/lung.
  4. STERNUM: Stop at the sternum. Use heavy shears or Gigli Saw.
    CRITICAL: Tunnel fingers/forceps under sternum first to clear pericardium. SNAP the sternum transversely.
  5. RETRACT: Insert Finochietto Retractor.
    CRITICAL: Place handle DOWN towards feet (so it doesn't hit chin). Crank hard to open chest.
  6. PERICARDIUM: Use toothed forceps to pick up the pericardium. Make a Longitudinal cut (Sup to Inf). If access is poor, make a transverse cut along the diaphragm to create an "INVERTED T" incision.
    WARNING: Avoid Phrenic Nerves (Run laterally).
  7. DELIVER HEART: Insert two hands behind the heart. Scoop it out of the sac. "BIRTH THE HEART" (Luxate) so it sits outside the chest cavity for massage.
  8. AORTA: Retract left lung medially. Locate Descending Aorta (Posterior, against spine). Compress against spine with fist or apply Satinsky clamp.

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

"The Clamshell" Approach

  • Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins. Signs of Life.
  • Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins. Witnessed.
  • Consider: Is this futile? (Asystole > 20 mins).
1
INCISION (CLAMSHELL)
  • Stop CPR briefly. 5th Intercostal Space.
  • Incise: Mid-Axillary Line to Mid-Axillary Line.
  • Go Deep: Cut through intercostal muscles.
2
EXPOSURE
  • Cut Sternum with Gigli saw or Shears.
  • Lift chest wall. Open Pericardium (Longitudinal).
  • Avoid Phrenic Nerve (Runs on lateral pericardium).
3
ACTIONS (H.O.T.)
  • Heart: Evacuate tamponade. Internal Massage (Two hands).
  • Outflow: Plug holes (Foley/Finger/Staples).
  • Thorax: Clamp Aorta (Descending).
4
AORTIC CROSS CLAMP
  • Retract left lung medially. Identify Descending Aorta on spine.
  • Apply vascular clamp.
  • Alternatively: Compress aorta against spine with fist.
Reference: FPHC & RCEM Consensus Statement on Traumatic Cardiac Arrest (2025).

BURNS EMERGENCY
Escharotomy Walkthrough
Indications & Criteria
‚Ä¢ Circumferential full-thickness burns (leather-like, painless, white/charred).
‚Ä¢ Chest: High peak inspiratory pressures (>30), Decreased tidal volumes, Hypoxia, Hypercapnia.
‚Ä¢ Limbs: "The 5 Ps". Pain (on passive extension - unreliable if deep), Pallor, Paraesthesia, Pulselessness (Late sign!), Perishingly cold.
‚Ä¢ Caution: Check Carboxyhaemoglobin (COHb) levels.
Equipment
Diathermy: Preferred (cuts & coagulates simultaneously)
Scalpel: Size 10 or 22 (if no diathermy - will bleed)
Surgical Marker: Draw lines first (Crucial for landmarks)
Dressings: Kaltostat / Algisite (Haemostatic)
Bandages: Crepe/Coban (Loose application)
Adrenaline: Topical (1:1000) soaked gauze for bleeding
Incision Lines: Chest & Limbs
Incision Technique (Detailed)
  1. DEPTH: Incise through the eschar (dead burnt tissue) down to subcutaneous fat.
    STOP: Do not cut deep fascia or muscle.
  2. ENDPOINT: Edges must gape widely (release of tension). You should see bubbling fat. Tissue should feel soft to touch.
  3. CHEST ("Roman Breastplate"):
    - Vertical: Bilateral Anterior Axillary Lines (Armpit to waist).
    - Horizontal: Connect the tops (Clavicular line) and bottoms (Costal Margin line). Creates a square that "springs" open.
  4. UPPER LIMB:
    - Mid-Lateral: Shoulder to wrist.
    - Mid-Medial: Inner arm.
    WARNING (ELBOW): At the medial elbow, the Ulnar nerve is superficial. Deviate the cut ANTERIORLY (in front of) the medial epicondyle to avoid slicing the nerve.
  5. LOWER LIMB:
    - Mid-Lateral: Hip to ankle.
    - Mid-Medial: Inner leg.
    WARNING (KNEE): At the fibular head (lateral knee), the Common Peroneal nerve is superficial. Keep incisions POSTERIOR to the fibular head.

Escharotomy

Circumferential Burns

  • Chest: Ventilation compromise.
  • Limbs: Ischaemia (Pallor, Absent Doppler).
  • Procedure is painless in full-thickness burns (nerves destroyed).
1
CHEST ("BREASTPLATE")
  • Vertical: Bilateral Anterior Axillary Lines.
  • Transverse: Connect tops (Clavicle) & bottoms (Costal Margin).
  • Ensure chest wall releases/springs open.
2
UPPER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial: Shoulder to wrist.
  • Hand: Dorsal incisions over metacarpals. Mid-lateral fingers.
  • Avoid Ulnar Nerve: Pass ANTERIOR to Medial Epicondyle.
3
LOWER LIMBS
  • Mid-Lateral & Mid-Medial: Hip to ankle.
  • Avoid Common Peroneal: Pass POSTERIOR to Fibular Head.
Reference: LSEBN Guidelines (2025).

Chest Drain Walkthrough
Indications & Contraindications
‚Ä¢ Trauma: Haemothorax, Pneumothorax (if ventilated/unstable).
‚Ä¢ Medical: Empyema, Massive Effusion.
‚Ä¢ Contra: Coagulopathy (Relative), Diaphragmatic Hernia.
‚Ä¢ Note: In tension pneumothorax with arrest, needle decompress FIRST. This takes too long.
Equipment (Sterile Pack)
Chlorhexidine (2%) + Drape
Lidocaine 1% (10-20ml) + 21G/23G Needles
Scalpel (Size 10)
Kelly Forceps (Large Curved & Straight)
Tube: 28-32F (Trauma/Blood), 12-14F (Air)
1-0 Silk Suture (Heavy)
Underwater Seal Drain (Primed with water)
Triangle of Safety: Lat Dorsi, Pec Major, Axilla, Nipple
Detailed Technique
  1. POSITION: Supine. Arm abducted behind head ("Sunbathing"). Strap arm down.
  2. IDENTIFY TRIANGLE:
    - Anterior: Pec Major.
    - Posterior: Lat Dorsi.
    - Base: 5th Rib (Nipple line).
  3. LOCAL ANAESTHETIC: Bleb skin. Go perpendicular. Infiltrate intercostal muscles.
    CRITICAL: Aspirate air/fluid from PLEURA to confirm depth. Withdraw needle whilst injecting to numb track.
  4. INCISION: 3-4cm Transverse cut (parallel to ribs). Go through skin/fat.
  5. DISSECT: Use Large Curved Kellys. Close jaws. Push into muscle. Open jaws to spread (blunt dissect). Create a tunnel.
    PATH: Go over the UPPER BORDER of the rib below (Avoid Neurovascular bundle which hides under the rib).
  6. BREACH: Hold forceps near tip (control). Push firmly through parietal pleura ("POP"). Open jaws wide (2cm) to stretch hole.
  7. SWEEP: Remove forceps. Insert Index Finger immediately. Rotate 360¬∞.
    FEEL: Lung (smooth/squishy)? Adhesions? Liver/Spleen (Firm - you are too low!).
  8. INSERT: Clamp tube tip with Kellys. Guide into chest along finger track. Aim Apical (Air) or Basal (Blood). Connect to seal.

Chest Drain (Open)

Trauma / Haemothorax

1
SAFETY TRIANGLE
  • Lat Dorsi (Posterior). Pec Major (Anterior). Axilla (Superior).
  • Target: 5th ICS, Anterior Axillary Line.
2
INCISION & DISSECTION
  • Local Anaesthetic. 3cm Transverse incision.
  • Blunt dissection with Kellys.
  • Stay on Upper Border of Lower Rib to avoid Neurovascular Bundle.
3
PLEURAL ENTRY
  • Push forceps through pleura ("Pop").
  • Open forceps widely to create track.
  • Finger Sweep is mandatory (verify you are in chest, not abdomen).
4
INSERTION
  • Insert Large Bore Tube (28-32F).
  • Look for "Fogging" and "Swinging" in tube.
  • Connect to seal. Suture (Mattress or Stay). Tape. CXR.
Reference: BTS & RCEM Guidelines (2025).

Pericardiocentesis Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Non-Traumatic Tamponade: Viral, Malignancy, Uraemia, Post-MI.
‚Ä¢ Signs (Beck's Triad): Hypotension, Distended Neck Veins, Muffled Heart Sounds.
‚Ä¢ POCUS: Circumferential fluid + RV Diastolic Collapse (specific).
‚Ä¢ Caution: In Trauma, blood is clotted -> Thoracotomy usually required.
Equipment
Pericardiocentesis Kit OR
Central Line Kit: Use the long 16G Cannula (Grey)
20ml Syringe
3-Way Tap (Crucial for aspiration)
Ultrasound Machine (Phased Array Probe)
Sterile Probe Cover + Sterile Gel
ECG Monitor (Clip for V1 lead if blind)
Choose Your Approach (Echo Guided)
Principle: Select site where fluid is closest to skin (<1cm) and pocket is largest.
  1. Apical (Preferred):
    - Landmark: 1-2cm Lateral to Apex Beat (5th-7th ICS).
    - Angle: Aim towards Right Shoulder. Advance over Superior Border of rib.
  2. Sub-Xiphoid (Standard):
    - Landmark: 1cm inferior to Left Xiphocostal angle.
    - Angle: 15-30¬∞ (Shallow). Aim towards Left Shoulder.
  3. Parasternal (Rescue):
    - Landmark: 5th Left ICS close to sternal margin.
    - Angle: Perpendicular to skin.
    - Caution: Internal Thoracic Vessels.
Choose Approach based on best Echo Window

Pericardiocentesis

Tamponade Decompression

  • Beck's Triad (Hypotension, Muffled Sounds, JVP).
  • POCUS: Fluid + RV Diastolic Collapse.
  • In Trauma -> Consider Thoracotomy.
1
CHOOSE APPROACH (ECHO GUIDED)
  • Apical: Lateral to apex. Shortest path. Seals well.
  • Sub-Xiphoid: Below xiphisternum. Aim Left Shoulder.
  • Parasternal: 5th ICS. Perpendicular. Watch for vessels.
2
EQUIPMENT HACK
  • No Kit? Open Central Line pack.
  • Use the 16G (Grey) Cannula.
  • Attach 3-Way Tap directly to cannula.
  • Advantage: You can leave the plastic cannula in situ as a drain.
3
PROCEDURE
  • Advance under Ultrasound guidance. Aspirate constantly.
  • Verification: Inject 2ml Agitated Saline (Look for bubbles).
  • Clotting: Fluid on gauze should NOT clot.
  • Slide catheter off needle. Connect 3-way tap. Drain.
Reference: ESC Guidelines 2015 / RCEM Guidance.
CARDIAC EMERGENCY
CARDIAC EMERGENCY
Pacing Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Unstable Bradycardia: < 40 bpm + Adverse Signs (Shock, Syncope, Ischaemia, Heart Failure).
‚Ä¢ Unresponsive to Atropine (500mcg-3mg) / Glycopyrrolate.
‚Ä¢ High Risk Blocks: Mobitz II, Complete Heart Block (CHB) with broad complex, Ventricular Pause > 3s.
Equipment
Defibrillator (Zoll/Philips) with Pacer mode
Pacing Pads: Ensure good skin contact
ECG Electrodes: 3-Lead must be attached
Sedation: Ketamine (0.5mg/kg) or Midazolam
Analgesia: Fentanyl (Pacing is painful)
Anterior (Precordium) & Posterior (Left Scapula)
Step-by-Step Configuration
  1. PLACEMENT: Anterior-Posterior (AP) is superior for capture.
    - Ant: Left sternal edge (Precordium).
    - Post: Left scapula region (Sandwich the heart).
  2. LEADS: Connect 3-lead ECG. Verify R-wave sensing (markers on screen). Pacer needs to "see" intrinsic beat to work.
  3. MODE: Turn dial to PACER. Select Demand mode.
  4. RATE: Set to 70-80 bpm.
  5. OUTPUT (Current): Start at 30 mA. Increase by 10 mA increments.
  6. ELECTRICAL CAPTURE: Look for a Pacer Spike followed immediately by a broad QRS and T wave.
  7. MECHANICAL CAPTURE: Palpate Femoral or Radial pulse.
    DO NOT trust Carotid (Skeletal muscle twitching mimics pulse).
    DO NOT trust SpO2 trace (Electrical interference).
  8. THRESHOLD: Identify lowest mA with capture. Set final output to Threshold + 10 mA (Safety Margin).

External Pacing

Unstable Bradycardia

  • Symptomatic Bradycardia unresponsive to drugs.
  • Sedation: Ketamine/Midazolam. Pacing is extremely painful (skeletal muscle contraction).
1
SETUP
  • Pads: Anterior-Posterior is superior to Anterior-Lateral.
  • Ensure 3-Lead ECG is connected (Pacer needs to sense R waves).
  • Select "Pacer". Rate: 70-80 bpm.
2
CAPTURE (mA)
  • Start 30 mA. Increase by 10 mA steps.
  • Look for Electrical Capture: Pacer Spike -> Broad QRS.
  • Note: Skeletal muscle twitching ‚â† Capture. Look at the ECG.
3
CONFIRMATION
  • Mechanical Capture: Palpate Femoral Pulse.
  • Do not use Carotid (muscle twitching mimics pulse).
  • Set current 10 mA above capture threshold (Safety Margin).
Reference: Resus Council UK ALS 2021.

Subclavian Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Hypovolaemic Shock (Vein remains patent due to fibrous attachments).
‚Ä¢ Neck trauma / C-Collar preventing Internal Jugular access.
‚Ä¢ Contraindications: Coagulopathy (Cannot compress artery), Contralateral pneumothorax (Risk of bilateral pneumothorax).
Equipment
Central Line Kit (Triple Lumen)
Sterile Gown, Gloves, Large Drapes
Chlorhexidine
Lidocaine 1%
Saline Flushes (x3) + Bungs
Suture (Silk) + Scalpel
Landmark Technique
  1. POSITION: 15¬∞ Trendelenburg (Head down).
    KEY STEP: Place rolled towel vertically between shoulder blades. Retract shoulders downwards. Turn head away.
  2. LANDMARK: Identify the junction of the Medial 1/3 and Middle 1/3 of the Clavicle.
  3. LOCAL: Infiltrate skin and periosteum of clavicle.
  4. INSERTION: Insert needle 1-2cm inferior to clavicle junction. Bevel Up.
  5. AIM: Aim towards the Sternal Notch (Place finger there to guide).
  6. TRAJECTORY: Keep syringe and needle PARALLEL TO THE FLOOR (Coronal plane).
    - Hit the clavicle first.
    - "Walk" the needle underneath the clavicle bone.
    - Aspirate constantly.
  7. ACCESS: Flash of dark blood -> Stabilise -> Wire (Must flow freely) -> Dilate -> Railraod.
  8. CHECK: Verify wire in vein (US) if available. Manometry. CXR Mandatory.
Landmarks: Medial/Mid 1/3 Clavicle -> Sternal Notch

Subclavian Line

Infraclavicular Landmark Approach

  • Use Ultrasound if available. Crash use only.
  • Right side preferred (Lower dome of pleura = less pneumothorax risk).
1
POSITION
  • Trendelenburg 15¬∞. Rolled towel between scapulae.
  • Head turned away.
2
LANDMARKS
  • Junction of Medial/Middle third of clavicle.
  • Insert 1cm below. Aim: Sternal Notch.
  • Keep flat: Needle must be parallel to floor to avoid lung.
3
SELDINGER
  • Venous Flash -> Stabilise. Insert Wire (should flow easily).
  • Check wire in correct place (US or Fluoroscopy).
  • Dilate -> Catheter -> Aspirate all ports -> Suture.
  • Mandatory CXR. (Check for Pneumothorax).
Reference: AAGBI Safe Vascular Access.
VASCULAR ACCESS
GI HAEMORRHAGE
Sengstaken Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Exsanguinating Variceal Haemorrhage (Oesophageal/Gastric).
‚Ä¢ Failure of endoscopic control or not available immediately.
‚Ä¢ Prerequisite: Patient MUST be intubated (RSI) to protect airway from blood/vomit.
‚Ä¢ Duration: Bridge to TIPS/Endoscopy (< 24 hours).
Equipment
Sengstaken-Blakemore or Minnesota Tube
50ml Syringe (Catheter Tip) + Spigots/Clamps
Lubricant (Lots)
1 Litre Saline Bag (For traction weight)
Giving set (To hang the weight)
Manometer (if inflating oesophageal balloon)
Critical Safety Steps
  1. TEST: Inflate Gastric & Oesophageal balloons underwater to check for bubbles/leaks. Deflate fully.
  2. INSERT: Lubricate tube. Insert via mouth (or nose if Minnesota). Advance to 50cm mark at lips.
  3. ASPIRATE: Aspirate gastric port. Confirm acidic pH or gastric content.
  4. TEST INFLATE: Inflate Gastric Balloon with 50ml Air ONLY.
  5. X-RAY CHECK: MANDATORY. Stop here. Get a portable CXR. Confirm balloon is clearly below diaphragm.
  6. FULL INFLATION: Once confirmed, inflate Gastric Balloon to 250ml total (Add 200ml). Clamp tube securely.
  7. TRACTION: Pull back gently until resistance (Balloon hits Gastro-Oesophageal Junction). Attach 1kg weight (1L Saline) via pulley/giving set.
  8. OESOPHAGEAL BALLOON: Only inflate if bleeding continues. 30-40mmHg. Use Manometer.
Gastric Balloon (Below Diaphragm) & Traction

Sengstaken / Minnesota

Uncontrollable Variceal Bleed

  • Intubate First. Mandatory.
  • Test balloons underwater. Lubricate well.
1
INSERTION & SAFETY
  • Insert to 50cm.
  • Inflate Gastric Balloon with 50ml Air ONLY.
  • STOP & CXR. Confirm balloon is below diaphragm.
  • If confirmed: Inflate to 250ml total. Clamp.
Warning: Inflating in oesophagus = Rupture/Death.
2
TRACTION
  • Gently pull back until resistance felt (Balloon hits GOJ).
  • Apply 1kg Traction (e.g. 1L Saline bag).
  • Hang bag over IV pole/bed end.
3
OESOPHAGEAL BALLOON
  • Rescue Only. Use only if bleeding continues despite traction.
  • Inflate to 30-40 mmHg (Use Manometer).
  • Deflate every 6-12h to prevent necrosis.
Reference: BSG Guidelines 2025.
GI HAEMORRHAGE
OPHTHALMIC EMERGENCY
Canthotomy Walkthrough
Indications & Diagnosis
‚Ä¢ Retrobulbar Haematoma: Bleeding behind globe causing Orbital Compartment Syndrome.
‚Ä¢ Signs: Proptosis (Bulging), Rock Hard Eye, 'Cherry Red' macula (late), Afferent Pupillary Defect (APD), Loss of Vision.
‚Ä¢ IOP: > 40 mmHg.
‚Ä¢ Action: Lateral Canthotomy & Cantholysis.
‚Ä¢ Rule: Do NOT wait for CT. If IOP high + Proptosis -> CUT.
Equipment
Lidocaine 1% or 2% with Adrenaline
2ml Syringe + Orange Needle (25G)
Irrigation Saline
Mosquito Forceps (Straight)
Small Sharp Scissors (Iris/Tenotomy)
Toothed Forceps (Adson)
Step-by-Step Procedure
  1. PREP: Irrigate debris. Inject 1ml LA into lateral canthus (Aim away from globe).
  2. CRUSH: Apply Mosquito forceps horizontally across the lateral canthus (Lateral Palpebral Raphe). Compress for 1 min to reduce bleeding.
  3. CANTHOTOMY (Skin): Remove forceps. Use scissors to cut the crushed skin/orbicularis muscle horizontally (1cm) towards orbital rim.
  4. CANTHOLYSIS (Tendon):
