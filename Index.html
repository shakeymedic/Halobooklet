How to use this guide
1. The Two-Page System
Every procedure consists of two sequential pages:

Page A: The Walkthrough (Preparation)
Use this during the "Kit Dump" phase. It includes specific equipment checklists, anatomy notes, and detailed setup instructions. One person should read this aloud while the team prepares.

Page B: The Flowchart (Action)
Use this during the procedure itself. Designed for high-stress cognitive processing: high contrast, minimal text, and clear steps.
2. Safety
Equipment Checklists: Use the check-boxes to ensure closed-loop communication (e.g., "Scalpel?" - "Check").
Warnings: Critical safety stops (e.g., gastric balloon inflation volumes) are highlighted in RED boxes.

OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY

Resuscitative Hysterotomy

Perimortem C-Section (>20 Weeks)

!
GOAL: DELIVERY < 5 MINS
  • Indication: Maternal Arrest. Uterus ‚â• Umbilicus.
  • Location: Operate on trolley. DO NOT move to theatre.
1
CPR MODIFICATIONS
  • Manual Uterine Displacement to LEFT.
  • Splash Chlorhexidine. Prepare Shears.
2
THE INCISION
  • Vertical Midline: Xiphisternum to Pubis.
  • Incise deep through skin/fat. Separate muscles.
3
UTERINE ENTRY
  • Vertical incision in uterus. Lift wall away.
  • Extend with Shears. Deliver Baby.
4
POST-DELIVERY
  • Manual Placenta removal. Close uterus.
  • Drugs: Oxytocin 10iu, Abx, TXA 1g.
Reference: Resuscitation Council UK (2025 Update).

Principles

  1. Intubate: Right Main Bronchus if possible.

TRAUMA ARREST

Resuscitative Thoracotomy

"The Clamshell" Approach

!
INDICATIONS
  • Penetrating: Arrest < 15 mins. Signs of Life.
  • Blunt: Arrest < 10 mins. Witnessed.
1
INCISION (CLAMSHELL)
  • Stop CPR briefly. 5th Intercostal Space.
  • Incise: Mid-Axillary Line to Mid-Axillary Line.
2
EXPOSURE
  • Cut Sternum with Gigli saw or Shears.
  • Lift chest wall. Open Pericardium (Longitudinal).
3
ACTIONS
  • Evacuate clots. Deliver heart.
  • Control Bleeding: Finger / Staples / Foley.
4
AORTIC CROSS CLAMP
  • Retract left lung medially. Identify Aorta.
  • Apply vascular clamp.
Reference: FPHC & RCEM Consensus Statement on Traumatic Cardiac Arrest (2025).

Pacing Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Symptomatic Bradycardia unresponsive to drugs.
‚Ä¢ Mobitz II or Complete Heart Block.
Equipment
Defib/Pacer Unit
Pacing Pads
ECG Electrodes (3-lead)
Sedation (Ketamine/Midaz)
Steps
  1. Pads: Anterior-Posterior is best.
  2. Mode: Select "Pacer". Rate 70-80.
  3. Current: Start 30mA. Increase until Electrical Capture (Spike+QRS).
  4. Confirm: Check Femoral pulse (Mechanical Capture).
  5. Safety: Set current +10mA above threshold.
CARDIAC EMERGENCY

External Pacing

Unstable Bradycardia

!
INDICATIONS
  • Symptomatic Bradycardia unresponsive to drugs.
  • Mobitz II or CHB.
  • Sedation: Ketamine/Midazolam (Painful).
1
SETUP
  • Pads: Anterior-Posterior. Connect ECG.
  • Select "Pacer". Rate: 70-80 bpm.
2
CAPTURE (mA)
  • Start 30 mA. Increase gradually.
  • Look for Electrical Capture: Spike + Broad QRS.
3
CONFIRMATION
  • Mechanical Capture: Palpate Femoral Pulse.
  • Set current 10 mA above threshold.

Sengstaken Walkthrough
Indications
‚Ä¢ Life-threatening Variceal Haemorrhage.
‚Ä¢ Uncontrolled by endoscopy/drugs.
‚Ä¢ Intubate First.
Equipment
Tube (Sengstaken/Minnesota)
50ml Syringe (Catheter tip)
Strong Clamps (x2)
Lubricant
1L Saline Bag (Weight)
Technique
  1. Check: Inflate balloons underwater. Deflate.
  2. Insert: To 50cm. Aspirate stomach.
  3. Test: 50ml Air -> Gastric Balloon.
  4. X-Ray: CONFIRM position below diaphragm.
  5. Inflate: 250ml Air -> Gastric Balloon. Clamp.
  6. Traction: Pull back. 1kg weight.
GI HAEMORRHAGE

Sengstaken / Minnesota

Uncontrollable Variceal Bleed

1
PREPARATION
  • Intubate First. Mandatory.
  • Test balloons underwater. Lubricate.
2
INSERTION & SAFETY
  • Insert to 50cm.
  • Inflate Gastric Balloon with 50ml Air ONLY.
  • STOP & CXR. Confirm balloon below diaphragm.
  • If confirmed: Inflate to 250ml. Clamp.
Warning: Oesophageal inflation = Rupture/Death.
3
TRACTION
  • Gently pull back until resistance felt.
  • Apply 1kg Traction (e.g. 1L Saline bag).
4
OESOPHAGEAL BALLOON
  • Rescue Only. Use if bleeding continues.
  • Inflate to 30-40 mmHg (Manometer).
Reference: BSG Guidelines 2025.

Steps

OPHTHALMIC EMERGENCY

Lateral Canthotomy

Retrobulbar Haematoma

!
INDICATIONS
  • Proptosis + Trauma + Decreased VA.
  • IOP > 40 mmHg. Time is Vision.
1
PREPARE
  • Irrigate eye. Local Anaesthetic w/ Adrenaline.
  • Aim needle away from globe.
2
CRUSH & CUT
  • Crush lateral canthus with Mosquito Forceps (1 min).
  • Cut the crushed tissue (Skin & Orbicularis).
3
CANTHOLYSIS
  • Pull lower lid down to reveal Inferior Crus.
  • "Strum" and Cut Inferior Crus.
  • Re-check IOP. If high -> Cut Superior Crus.
Reference: RCEM & Royal College of Ophthalmologists Consensus (2025).

LAST Walkthrough
Signs
‚Ä¢ Metallic taste, Tinnitus, Agitation.
‚Ä¢ Seizures.
‚Ä¢ CVS collapse (Bradycardia, Broad QRS, Asystole).
Lipid Rescue Kit
Intralipid 20% (x3 bags)
50ml Syringe
Giving Sets
Action
  1. Stop Injection. Call Help.
  2. Airway: Intubate. Hyperventilate (pH > 7.45).
  3. Bolus: 1.5ml/kg (approx 100ml). Repeat x2 if needed.
  4. Infusion: 15ml/kg/hr.
  5. CPR: Continue for >1 hour if needed.
ANAESTHETIC EMERGENCY

LAST (Lipid Rescue)

Local Anaesthetic Toxicity

!
RECOGNITION
  • CNS: Tingling, metallic taste, seizure.
  • CVS: Bradycardia, Broad QRS, Arrest.
  • Action: STOP INJECTION. Call for Lipid.
1
AIRWAY & CPR
  • Intubate. Hyperventilate (alkalosis).
  • Prolonged CPR. Avoid Lidocaine.
2
INTRALIPID 20% (BOLUS)
  • Bolus: 1.5 ml/kg (approx 100ml).
  • Repeat q5min if unstable (Max 3).
3
INTRALIPID 20% (INFUSION)
  • Rate: 15 ml/kg/hour (approx 1000ml/hr).
  • Double rate if unstable.
Reference: AAGBI Safety Guideline 2023.

Surgical Airway (eFONA)

Can't Intubate, Can't Oxygenate (CICO)

‚ö†Ô∏è GRAB KIT FROM DIFFICULT AIRWAY TROLLEY
1
DECLARE EMERGENCY
  • Call out: "CICO. Proceeding to Emergency Front of Neck Airway."
  • Action: 100% Oxygen to face. Extend Neck.
2
LARYNGEAL HANDSHAKE
  • Grasp Hyoid and Thyroid. Slide finger to Cricothyroid Membrane.
  • Plan: Scalpel - Bougie - Tube.
3
INCISION (SCALPEL)
  • Transverse Stab through CTM.
  • Turn Blade 90¬∞ (Sharp edge caudal).
  • DO NOT REMOVE SCALPEL. Swap hands if needed.
4
RAILROAD
  • Slide Bougie down the blade into trachea.
  • Remove Scalpel. Railroad 6.0 ETT.
  • Twist tube. Inflate cuff.
5
CONFIRMATION
  • Connect BVM. Confirm EtCO2.
  • Suction trachea. Secure tube.
Reference: Difficult Airway Society (DAS) 2025 Guidelines.
